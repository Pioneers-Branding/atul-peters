Programme at a Glance

A Fellowship Built for the Real World

India is facing a growing epidemic of obesity and Type 2 Diabetes. Every year, thousands of patients opt for bariatric and metabolic surgery — yet there is a critical shortage of trained nutritionists and dieticians who understand the unique pre-operative and post-operative needs of these patients.

The Fellowship in Bariatric & Metabolic Nutrition addresses this gap by providing comprehensive, hands-on training at one of the country's leading bariatric surgery centers — equipping nutrition professionals with the expertise to manage patients across the entire surgical journey.

Curriculum

What You Will Learn

A structured, week-by-week curriculum that builds your expertise progressively — from foundational knowledge to advanced case management.

M1
Month 1

Foundations of Bariatric & Metabolic Nutrition

  • Obesity epidemiology & pathophysiology
  • Types of bariatric procedures (RYGB, Sleeve, MGB)
  • Pre-op assessment & patient optimization
  • Pre-op liver-shrinking diet protocols
  • Anthropometry & body composition analysis
  • Setting SMART nutrition goals
  • Introduction to the bariatric MDT workflow
  • Patient communication & motivational interviewing
M2
Month 2

Post-Operative Dietary Management & Casework

  • Post-op diet progression: clear liquids → solids
  • Protein-first nutrition strategy
  • Vitamin & mineral supplementation
  • Managing dumping syndrome & food intolerances
  • Live OPD case discussions (50+ patients)
  • Diet plan design & review (15 cases)
  • Pregnancy after bariatric surgery
  • Adolescent & geriatric considerations
M3
Month 3

Long-Term Follow-Up, Research & Assessment

  • Weight regain: causes & interventions
  • Behavioural therapy & relapse prevention
  • Building a bariatric nutrition practice
  • Telemedicine & remote follow-up
  • Research methodology & case study write-up
  • Journal club & evidence-based practice
  • Final case presentation
  • Qualifying exam & viva voce

Is the fellowship paid or unpaid?

This is a paid, self-funded program. There is no stipend, and fellows are not employees of Max Hospital. Detailed fee structure is shared on request and during the interview round.

Can I apply if I am in my final year of M.Sc. Nutrition?

Yes, final-year students are eligible to apply, provided they will have completed their degree before the fellowship start date. You will need a provisional degree certificate and a letter from your Head of Department.

Is accommodation provided?

Accommodation is not provided by the hospital. However, our team can share verified paying-guest and rental options near the hospital, and incoming fellows are connected with current fellows to coordinate stay.

Will I get hands-on patient interaction?

Yes. From week 2 onwards, fellows sit in on OPD consultations, take diet histories under supervision, design personalised diet plans, and present cases during weekly MDT meetings.

What is the assessment format?

Assessment is continuous — case logs, weekly quizzes, a mid-program viva, and a final case presentation. The qualifying exam at the end of month 3 includes a written paper and a practical viva voce.

Is this degree recognised by UGC or any university?

This is a certificate fellowship awarded by Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital under the supervision of Dr. Atul Peters. It is not a UGC-recognised degree but is widely respected in the bariatric and metabolic surgery community as the most hands-on nutrition fellowship in India.
