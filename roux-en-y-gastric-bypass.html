Procedure Overview

What is Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass?

Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (RYGB) is a metabolic surgery with dual mechanisms: It works through restrictive changes (smaller stomach pouch) and, more importantly, metabolic changes (gut hormone reprogramming). Performed for over 60 years, RYGB is widely considered the "Gold Standard" in metabolic surgery for its ability to achieve remission of Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and other obesity-related diseases.

RYGB works through a combination of restrictive and metabolic changes, helping patients eat smaller meals while also influencing gut hormones that regulate hunger, satiety, blood sugar and metabolism. In practice, the procedure works in three powerful, simultaneous ways: First, the surgeon creates a small stomach pouch — about the size of an egg (30–50 ml) — using surgical staplers, which immediately restricts the amount of food you can eat in a single sitting. Second, the small intestine is carefully divided and rerouted, bypassing 100–170 cm of the upper intestine. Third, and most importantly for long-term success, the bypass reroutes food before it reaches the duodenum and proximal jejunum, dramatically altering gut hormone signaling — including GLP-1, PYY and ghrelin — to reduce hunger, enhance satiety, regulate blood sugar, and reset the body's weight set-point.

The result is a procedure that delivers the most durable weight loss and the highest rates of Type 2 diabetes remission of any bariatric operation, while also resolving or improving a wide spectrum of obesity-related conditions — from hypertension and sleep apnoea to PCOS and infertility. Performed laparoscopically or with robotic precision at Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital, RYGB is typically completed in 45–90 minutes with a 2–3 night hospital stay.

Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (RYGB) is a metabolic surgery with dual mechanisms — restrictive and hormonal — that has helped thousands of Delhi and international patients achieve lasting weight loss and remission of Type 2 Diabetes. How RYGB Differs from Other Surgeries

Unlike purely restrictive procedures such as Sleeve Gastrectomy, RYGB reroutes the gastrointestinal tract to deliver food directly to the mid-small intestine. This bypass alters the release of gut hormones — GLP-1, PYY, oxyntomodulin and ghrelin — producing dramatic improvements in insulin sensitivity, satiety and the body's weight set-point.

Long-Term Outcomes (10–20 Year Data)

Published meta-analyses and the landmark SOS (Swedish Obese Subjects) study show that RYGB patients maintain an average of ~70–80% excess weight loss at 10 years, with sustained remission of Type 2 Diabetes in ~60% of cases at 15 years. Cardiovascular events and cancer risk are significantly reduced.

Why Choose RYGB Over Sleeve Gastrectomy?

Patients with BMI ≥ 35 plus Type 2 Diabetes, severe reflux or metabolic syndrome are particularly well-suited to RYGB. Sleeve gastrectomy may be preferred for younger patients with lower BMI and no reflux. The choice is always made jointly between patient and surgeon.

The Procedure

How Gastric Bypass Works

A precise, minimally invasive surgery that creates a new pathway for digestion

01

Pre-Surgery Preparation

A comprehensive team evaluation including body composition analysis, dietary counseling, physician review, pulmonologist and cardiologist clearance, and psychiatric assessment. Thromboembolism prophylaxis is administered 12 hours before surgery.

02

Positioning & Anesthesia

You are placed in the reverse Trendelenberg position under general anesthesia. This specialized positioning allows optimal access to the upper abdomen for the surgical team.

03

Small Incisions & Robotic Port Placement

The surgeon makes 3–4 small incisions (5–12mm) in the abdomen. These tiny ports accommodate the laparoscopic camera and specialized instruments. In 99% of cases, incisions are closed with medical glue — no stitches or bandages required.

Robotic (da Vinci): 5–6 ports, 10x 3D vision, sub-mm precision via tremor-free robotic arms.

04

Creating the Pouch

A small gastric pouch (about 30-50ml — the size of an egg) is created at the top of the stomach using surgical staplers. A calibration tube ensures precise sizing. The remainder of the stomach is separated but left in place.

05

Bypass the Intestine

The small intestine is divided, and 100-170 cm is bypassed. The lower portion of the intestine is then connected directly to the new stomach pouch, allowing food to skip the bypassed segment.

06

Reconnection & Closure

The Y-shaped reconnection allows bile and pancreatic juices to mix with food further down the intestine. The new anatomy is secured with staples or sutures, and the small incisions are sealed with glue.

Frequently Asked

RYGB Questions Answered

9 of the most common questions about Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass, answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

What exactly is done during a Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass?

The surgeon creates a small (30 ml) egg-sized stomach pouch and reroutes the small intestine to bypass 100–170 cm of the upper gut, forming the Y-shaped reconnection. It is performed laparoscopically or with the da Vinci robot through 3–6 small incisions. Operative time is typically 45-90 minutes and hospital stay is 1-2 nights.

How is RYGB different from sleeve gastrectomy and mini gastric bypass?

RYGB combines restriction with metabolic hormone reprogramming, giving the highest diabetes remission (~85%) and reliable reflux improvement. Sleeve gastrectomy removes ~75% of the stomach but is irreversible. Mini gastric bypass has a simpler single-loop anatomy but slightly less long-term data.

What is the BMI requirement for gastric bypass in India?

In India, RYGB is recommended for BMI ≥ 35 kg/m² with or without comorbidities, or BMI ≥ 32.5 kg/m² with Type 2 Diabetes or other obesity-related conditions. For Asian patients these thresholds may be lowered by 2.5 points per IFSO-Asia guidelines.

Can I have RYGB if I have Type 2 Diabetes?

Yes — Type 2 Diabetes is the single strongest indication for RYGB. Studies show ~85% remission rates, often within days of surgery, independent of weight loss, due to changes in gut hormones GLP-1, PYY and ghrelin.

How many days will I need to stay in hospital after RYGB?

Most patients stay 1–2 nights at Max Smart Hospital, Saket. Laparoscopic cases can sometimes be discharged the next day. Robotic cases follow the same protocol. Return to desk work is typically within 1–2 weeks.

What is the post-RYGB diet plan?

The diet progresses in stages: clear liquids (Weeks 1-2), full liquids and protein shakes (Week 3), pureed and soft foods (Weeks 4-5), and finally a return to solid foods (Week 6+). Lifelong habits require small portions, high protein, and daily vitamin supplementation.

What is the cost of RYGB in Delhi and is it covered by insurance?

Indicative cost at Max Smart Hospital, Saket is INR 4.5L – 6.5L for self-pay. Most IRDA-approved bariatric covers and cashless networks (Max, MediAssist, MDIndia, Paramount) reimburse the procedure when BMI and comorbidity criteria are met. EMI and 0% financing options are also available.

What are the main risks and complications of RYGB?

Early risks include bleeding, anastomotic leak (~1%) and DVT. Late risks include marginal ulcer, dumping syndrome, vitamin deficiencies (B12, iron, calcium, vitamin D), and internal hernia. Lifelong follow-up and supplementation minimise these. Dr. Peters has a major complication rate of < 2%.

Will I regain weight after gastric bypass surgery?

Most patients maintain 70–80% of their excess weight loss at 10 years. Weight regain of 5–10% can occur around 18–24 months if dietary and exercise guidelines are not followed. Long-term success depends on adherence to follow-up, protein intake and physical activity.
