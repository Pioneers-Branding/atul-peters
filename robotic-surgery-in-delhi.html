What is Robotic-Assisted Surgery?

Robotic surgery is the most advanced form of minimally invasive (keyhole) surgery available today. It is important to note that the robot does not perform the surgery autonomously; instead, it is directly controlled by an expert surgeon.

Surgeon Control

The surgeon sits at a specialized console, viewing a highly magnified, 3D high-definition image of the surgical site. The system translates the surgeon's exact hand, wrist, and finger movements into precise, micro-movements of the instruments inside the patient's body in real-time.

Unmatched Precision

The Da Vinci robotic arms feature EndoWrist technology, providing a range of motion greater than the human hand, complete with tremor filtration. This allows for unparalleled precision, especially in complex surgeries or tight anatomical spaces deep within the pelvis or abdomen.

Step-by-Step

How Da Vinci Robotic Surgery Works

Understanding the three core components of the Da Vinci Surgical System.

1. The Surgeon Console

This is where Dr. Peters sits during the operation. He views a high-definition 3D image of the surgical site that is magnified up to 10 times. His hand, wrist, and finger movements at the console are translated in real-time to the robotic instruments.

2. The Patient Cart

Positioned near the patient, the cart holds the camera and the surgical instruments. These highly advanced robotic arms mimic the surgeon's movements but with greater range of motion, allowing for extreme dexterity in tight spaces.

3. The Vision Cart

This cart makes communication between the components possible and supports the 3D high-definition vision system. It also provides a widescreen view of the surgery for the rest of the assisting operating room team.

Why Choose Robotic

The Da Vinci Advantage

For many complex procedures, robotic surgery offers significant benefits over traditional open surgery and standard laparoscopy.

Enhanced Visualization

The 3D HD camera provides a highly magnified, true depth-of-field view, allowing the surgeon to see tiny blood vessels and nerves with absolute clarity, ensuring safer dissections.

Quicker Return to Life

Because incisions are tiny and tissue manipulation is highly precise, patients experience significantly less post-operative pain, leading to a faster return to normal daily activities.

Reduced Complications

The minimally invasive nature of robotic surgery translates to reduced blood loss, a lower risk of surgical site infections, and minimized scarring compared to open surgical methods.

Surgical Applications

Common Robotic Procedures

Dr. Atul Peters utilizes the Da Vinci system for a wide range of complex gastrointestinal and bariatric conditions.

Robotic Bariatric Surgery

Ideal for highly obese individuals or those requiring complex revision bariatric surgeries. The robot offers superior ergonomics and precision when navigating thick abdominal walls, making procedures like Gastric Bypass and Sleeve Gastrectomy safer and more effective.

Robotic Hernia Repair (eTEP/TAR)

For complex abdominal wall reconstructions and large ventral hernias, robotic surgery allows for precise layer-by-layer dissection and suturing of mesh in the extraperitoneal space, ensuring a robust repair with minimal recurrence risk.

Complex Gallbladder & GI Surgery

When dealing with severe inflammation, dense adhesions from previous surgeries, or complex biliary anatomy, the Da Vinci robot's 3D HD visualization and articulating instruments provide the critical precision needed for a safe cholecystectomy or GI resection.

Hiatal Hernia & Anti-Reflux Surgery

Operating near the diaphragm requires immense precision. Robotic-assisted Nissen Fundoplication for GERD and hiatal hernias ensures delicate handling of the esophagus and stomach, leading to excellent functional outcomes and rapid recovery.

Post-Op Experience

What to Expect During Recovery

Robotic surgery dramatically reduces the physical toll of surgery, allowing for a much faster return to your daily life.

0-24 Hours (Hospital)

Most patients are out of bed and walking within a few hours of surgery. Due to the minimal incisions, pain is well-controlled. Depending on the complexity of the procedure (like bariatric or complex hernia), discharge often occurs within 24 to 48 hours.

Days 1-7 (Home)

You will likely experience only mild soreness. Patients are encouraged to take light walks to promote circulation. Most people can comfortably return to desk work, driving, and normal daily routines within a week.

Weeks 2-6 (Full Activity)

The tiny 8mm incisions heal rapidly, leaving virtually invisible scars. By week 4 to 6, patients can typically resume all strenuous activities, including heavy lifting and intense exercise, without restriction.
