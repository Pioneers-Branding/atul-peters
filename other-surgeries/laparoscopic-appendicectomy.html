Understanding Appendicitis

Why An Inflamed Appendix Requires Urgent Surgery

The appendix is a small, blind-ended tube attached to the cecum (the beginning of the large intestine). When its lumen becomes blocked by hardened stool (fecalith), lymphoid swelling, or infection, pressure builds rapidly inside the organ.

The Danger of Perforation (Rupture)

As bacterial infection escalates, the blood supply to the appendix is choked off, causing gangrene. Within **24 to 72 hours of symptom onset**, an untreated appendix can burst, spilling bacteria and pus throughout the abdomen (peritonitis), requiring extensive emergency drainage.

The 4K Laparoscopic Keyhole Solution

Through 3 tiny keyhole incisions (one 10mm port at the navel and two 5mm working ports), Dr. Atul Peters inspects the abdomen with a 4K HD camera. The appendix and its blood vessels (mesoappendix) are carefully sealed using advanced harmonic energy or endoclips.

Protective Endobag Extraction

Once detached, the infected appendix is placed inside a sterile specimen bag (**Endobag**) before being removed through the navel port. This crucial step ensures that pus and bacteria never touch or contaminate the abdominal wall incision, keeping wound infection rates below 1%.

Common Questions

Get clear, evidence-based answers to the most frequently asked questions about Laparoscopic Appendicectomy surgery.

Ask Dr. Peters
Can appendicitis be treated with antibiotics alone without surgery?

While intravenous antibiotics can temporarily calm mild, early-stage appendiceal inflammation in select cases, medical therapy alone carries a **high risk of recurrent appendicitis (up to 30-40% within 1 year)** and potential progression to abscess or rupture. Surgical appendicectomy remains the international gold standard because it permanently removes the diseased organ, guaranteeing a 100% cure.

What happens if an inflamed appendix bursts (perforates)?

When an appendix ruptures, bacteria and fecal matter spill into the sterile abdominal cavity, causing a severe, life-threatening infection called **peritonitis** or localized pus collections (abscesses). This requires urgent surgical washout and drainage, prolonged IV antibiotics, and a longer hospital stay. Seeking medical evaluation immediately when abdominal pain begins prevents rupture.

Will I have digestive problems or weight gain after losing my appendix?

No. The appendix is a vestigial organ without an essential digestive function in older children and adults. Removing it causes **zero changes to your digestion, metabolism, weight, or immune system**. Once healed, you can eat spicy foods, fiber, and your normal diet without any restrictions.

How soon can I shower and bathe after laparoscopic surgery?

We use modern waterproof dressings over the 3 tiny keyhole incisions. You can take a quick shower **48 hours after surgery**, patting the dressings dry gently with a clean towel. Avoid soaking in a bathtub or swimming pool until your follow-up visit at 1 week.

Is emergency appendix surgery covered by health insurance in India?

Yes! Emergency and elective laparoscopic appendicectomy are covered under 100% of health insurance policies, corporate mediclaim, TPAs, and CGHS/ECHS schemes in India. Max Smart Hospital Saket has a dedicated 24/7 emergency desk ensuring immediate cashless processing.

When can I resume driving, gym workouts, and sports?

You can resume driving and routine non-strenuous desk work within **3 to 5 days** once you are no longer taking prescription pain medication. Light brisk walking is encouraged immediately. For heavy gym lifting, core abdominal exercises, running, or contact sports, we advise waiting **2 to 3 weeks** to allow your abdominal wall tissues to heal completely.
