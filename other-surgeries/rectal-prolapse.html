Understanding Prolapse

Why Rectal Prolapse Requires Anatomical Suspension

Rectal prolapse occurs when the ligaments and pelvic floor structures supporting the rectum stretch and collapse. This causes the entire full-thickness wall of the rectum to telescope inside out and protrude out through the anal opening.

Incontinence & Obstructed Defecation (ODS)

A prolapsed rectum stretches anal sphincter muscles, causing chronic stool leakage and mucus discharge (**Fecal Incontinence**). In hidden internal prolapse (intussusception), the folded rectal wall blocks stool passage, forcing patients to strain endlessly or use digital manipulation to empty bowel (**Obstructed Defecation Syndrome**).

Why Exercises Cannot Lift a Fallen Organ

Once ligaments have failed mechanically, Kegel exercises or diets cannot re-attach the rectum to the pelvic bone. Surgical rectopexy is required to physically lift and suspend the organ.

The Ventral Mesh Rectopexy (LVMR/RVMR) Breakthrough

Dr. Atul Peters specializes in **Ventral Mesh Rectopexy**—suturing a biocompatible mesh to the front of the rectum and anchoring it to the strong sacral promontory bone. Because lateral nerves are left untouched, bowel function improves immediately without nerve injury!

Pelvic Floor Conditions We Reconstruct

Dr. Atul Peters provides tailored minimal access and robotic rectopexy across all stages of rectal and pelvic organ prolapse.

01

Full-Thickness External Rectal Prolapse

The classic condition where the entire rectal wall protrudes several centimeters outside the anus during defecation or standing. We restore permanent suspension via **Ventral Mesh Rectopexy**.

02

Internal Rectal Intussusception (Hidden Prolapse)

The rectum telescopes inside itself without protruding outside the anus. This causes severe **Obstructed Defecation Syndrome (ODS)**, feeling of incomplete bowel emptying, and chronic straining.

03

Rectocele & Pelvic Organ Prolapse

When the front wall of the rectum bulges forward into the posterior vaginal wall. Ventral rectopexy reinforces the rectovaginal septum, curing both rectocele and vaginal vault descent.

04

Solitary Rectal Ulcer Syndrome (SRUS)

Chronic rectal bleeding and painful ulcers caused by internal intussusception repeatedly pushing rectal mucosa against the anal sphincter. Re-suspending the rectum allows ulcers to heal permanently.

Ask Dr. Peters
Why is Ventral Mesh Rectopexy superior to older posterior rectopexy?

Older posterior rectopexy required extensive cutting around the back and sides of the rectum, which frequently severed delicate lateral nerves and caused severe, permanent post-operative constipation in up to 50% of patients. **Ventral Mesh Rectopexy (LVMR/RVMR)** confines dissection strictly to the front (anterior) wall between the rectum and vagina/bladder. Because lateral nerves are never cut, it cures prolapse without causing constipation and actually improves bowel emptying!

Is surgical mesh safe in pelvic floor rectopexy?

Yes. In ventral mesh rectopexy, we use premium, lightweight, macroporous monofilament polypropylene or biological collagen grafts. The mesh is placed anteriorly and anchored to the sacral bone, well away from the anal sphincter. Your body's natural tissue grows into the mesh lattice within weeks, creating a reinforced biological ligament that holds the rectum suspended for life with exceptional safety.

How soon will stool leakage (incontinence) stop after surgery?

The mechanical protrusion of the rectum is eliminated immediately upon waking up from surgery. For patients who suffered from chronic fecal incontinence or mucus leakage due to stretched sphincters, bowel control and sphincter tone recover progressively over the first **4 to 8 weeks** as the anal muscles regain their normal resting tension without the prolapsing mass pushing through.

What is the difference between laparoscopic and robotic rectopexy?

Both are minimally invasive keyhole surgeries performed through 3 to 4 tiny incisions. While standard laparoscopy uses rigid instruments, **Robotic Rectopexy (da Vinci Xi System)** provides 10x magnified 3D HD vision and wristed instruments that rotate 360 degrees. The robotic system is uniquely advantageous when operating deep inside the narrow bony pelvis, allowing sub-millimeter precision when suturing the mesh to the rectum and sacrum.

Is rectal prolapse surgery covered by health insurance in India?

Yes! Rectal prolapse and internal intussusception causing obstructed defecation or incontinence are recognized functional anatomical disorders. Laparoscopic and robotic ventral mesh rectopexy is covered under 100% of health insurance policies, corporate mediclaim networks, TPAs, and CGHS/ECHS schemes in India. Max Smart Hospital Saket provides seamless cashless claim assistance.

When can I resume driving, office work, and gym exercises?

You can walk immediately and return to routine non-strenuous desk work and driving within **5 to 7 days**. For heavy gym lifting (> 10 kg), squatting, intense abdominal core exercises, running, or cycling, we advise waiting **4 to 6 weeks** to allow the sacral mesh suspension to integrate solidly into your pelvic bones.
