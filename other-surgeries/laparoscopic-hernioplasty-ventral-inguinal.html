Understanding Hernias

Why An Abdominal Hernia Requires Surgical Reinforcement

An abdominal hernia occurs when a tear or structural weakness develops in the muscular wall of your abdomen or groin. This allows internal fat or loops of intestine to push through, forming a noticeable lump or bulge that worsens when coughing, lifting, or standing.

The Risk of Incarceration & Strangulation

Hernias do not heal or shrink on their own. If a loop of intestine gets trapped inside the muscle tear (**Incarceration**) and its blood supply is cut off (**Strangulation**), the bowel can gangrene within hours. This is a life-threatening emergency requiring urgent bowel resection.

The eTEP & TAR Retro-Muscular Revolution

In older surgery, mesh was placed inside the abdomen touching bowel loops. Dr. Atul Peters specializes in **eTEP (extended Totally Extraperitoneal)** and **TAR (Transversus Abdominis Release)**—operating through keyholes *behind* the abdominal muscles without entering the peritoneal cavity!

Natural Intra-Abdominal Mesh Locking

By placing a large, lightweight biocompatible mesh in the sub-lay muscular pocket, natural abdominal pressure presses the muscles against the mesh, locking it in place without painful peritoneal tacks or nerve entrapment.

Common Questions

Get clear, evidence-based answers to the most frequently asked questions about Laparoscopic & Robotic Hernioplasty.

Ask Dr. Peters
Can an abdominal or groin hernia heal with exercise or a hernia belt?

No. A hernia is a physical tear or gap in the muscular wall of the abdomen or groin. Exercises, yoga, abdominal binders, or medications cannot stitch or heal a torn muscle plane. While a hernia belt may temporarily hold the bulge in during wear, it does not cure the defect and can cause skin irritation or scarring over time. Surgical repair with mesh is the only permanent medical cure.

What is the difference between eTEP/TAR and traditional open or IPOM surgery?

In older open surgery, a large cut is made through muscles and nerves. In older laparoscopic IPOM surgery, mesh was placed inside the abdominal cavity directly touching bowel loops. In our advanced **eTEP (extended Totally Extraperitoneal)** and **TAR** surgery, Dr. Atul Peters operates through keyhole ports *behind* the muscles in a protective retro-muscular pocket. This ensures **zero contact with bowel loops**, eliminates painful peritoneal tacks, and reduces recurrence to less than 1%.

Is prosthetic hernia mesh safe and will my body reject it?

Yes, surgical mesh is exceptionally safe and permanent. We use premium, lightweight, macro-porous, biocompatible polypropylene or hybrid meshes from world-leading medical manufacturers. Once placed in the sub-lay muscular space, your body's natural collagen grows into the mesh pores within weeks, creating a reinforced biological shield that prevents hernia recurrence for life without rejection.

When can I resume lifting weights, gym workouts, and sports?

You can walk immediately and return to routine desk work and driving within **5 to 7 days**. For heavy lifting (> 10 kg), intense gym lifting, core abdominal exercises, yoga, or competitive sports, we advise waiting **4 to 6 weeks** to allow your body's collagen to fully integrate into the mesh lattice.

Is laparoscopic and robotic hernioplasty covered by health insurance in India?

Yes! Under modern **IRDAI guidelines**, laparoscopic and robotic hernia repairs (inguinal, ventral, umbilical, and incisional) are medically necessary surgical procedures covered by 100% of health insurance policies, corporate mediclaim networks, TPAs, and CGHS/ECHS schemes in India. Max Smart Hospital Saket offers seamless cashless claim assistance.

What is the risk of the hernia coming back (recurrence)?

With traditional open surgery without mesh, recurrence rates exceeded 15%. With our advanced **eTEP and TAR sub-lay mesh techniques** performed by Dr. Atul Peters, the recurrence rate is **less than 1%**. Because the mesh is placed behind the muscles, natural abdominal pressure actually presses the muscles against the mesh, locking it securely in place for a lifetime.
