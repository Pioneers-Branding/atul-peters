Understanding Anal Fistula

Why Anal Fistulas Require Sphincter-Preserving Laser Care

An anal fistula is an abnormal infected tunnel connecting a clogged anal gland inside the rectum to the external skin around the anal verge. It causes chronic pain, swelling, and persistent discharge of pus or blood.

The Terror of Traditional Open Surgery

In traditional open surgery (fistulotomy), the surgeon must cut open the entire fistula tract. Because these tracts cross through the anal sphincter muscles that control bowel movements, cutting them carries a high risk of **Anal Incontinence** (permanent loss of stool and gas control).

The FiLaC (Fistula Laser Closure) Revolution

Dr. Atul Peters utilizes **FiLaC**—inserting a thin, flexible 360-degree radial diode laser fiber directly into the fistula tract from the outside pinhole opening without cutting or touching any sphincter muscles!

Internal Photothermal Sealing

As the laser fiber is slowly withdrawn, radial laser energy vaporizes infected granulation tissue and photothermally shrinks and welds the tract closed from within. The tract heals cleanly without raw open wounds or daily dressings.

Ask Dr. Peters
Why is FiLaC laser superior to open surgery or Kshar Sutra thread treatment?

Traditional open surgery cuts sphincter muscles (risking permanent incontinence) and leaves a large gaping wound requiring weeks of painful daily dressings. Kshar Sutra (medicated seton thread) requires multiple painful weekly thread tightenings over 3 to 6 months. **FiLaC radial laser surgery** seals the tract from inside out in a single 30-minute day-care procedure without cutting muscles, without open wounds, and with 100% sphincter preservation.

Can an anal fistula or hemorrhoids heal with ointments or home remedies?

No. An anal fistula is a physical tunnel lined with infected granulation tissue; creams, sitz baths, or antibiotics cannot seal or close this tunnel from within. Similarly, Grade III/IV hemorrhoids are prolapsed vascular cushions that will not shrink permanently with ointments. Laser proctology (FiLaC / LHP) is the only minimally invasive, definitive medical cure.

Will I have pain or bleeding during bowel movements after laser surgery?

Because there are no surgical cuts, stitches, or raw open wounds in the anal canal, postoperative pain and bleeding during bowel movements are dramatically reduced compared to open surgery. We prescribe stool softeners and recommend warm water sitz baths to make bathroom visits smooth and comfortable from Day 1.

What is the risk of the fistula coming back (recurrence)?

When performed by an experienced proctological surgeon like Dr. Atul Peters using high-precision radial emitting diode fibers and pre-operative MRI mapping (to identify all secondary tracts and internal openings), the success rate of FiLaC exceeds 90–95%, with minimal recurrence and zero incontinence.

Is laser fistula and hemorrhoid surgery covered by health insurance in India?

Yes! Under modern **IRDAI guidelines**, laser proctological surgeries (FiLaC for fistula, LHP for piles/hemorrhoids, and SiLaC for pilonidal sinus) are advanced medically indicated procedures covered by 100% of health insurance policies, corporate mediclaim networks, TPAs, and CGHS/ECHS schemes in India. Max Smart Hospital Saket provides seamless cashless claim assistance.

When can I resume driving, office work, and gym exercises?

You can walk immediately and return to non-strenuous desk work and driving within **2 to 3 days**. For heavy gym lifting, cycling, running, or swimming, we advise waiting **2 to 3 weeks** to allow the internal laser-sealed tract to consolidate completely.
