Understanding Hemorrhoids

Why Swollen Piles Require Advanced Cut-Free Care

Hemorrhoids (piles) are swollen vascular cushions in the lower anal canal. Chronic constipation, pregnancy, heavy lifting, and prolonged sitting cause these veins to engorge and stretch, leading to bleeding, itching, and painful protrusion (prolapse) outside the anus.

The Agony of Traditional Open Surgery

In older open hemorrhoidectomy, surgeons cut away hemorrhoid bundles with scalpels, leaving raw open wounds in the highly nerve-dense anal opening. Stool passing over these raw cuts causes excruciating pain for weeks and risks anal narrowing (stenosis).

Laser Hemorrhoidoplasty (LHP)

Dr. Atul Peters uses a specialized radial laser fiber inserted directly into the pile mass. Laser energy photothermally seals the feeding blood vessels from within, causing the pile to shrink and disappear naturally without a single surgical cut!

MIPH Stapled Hemorrhoidopexy

For large circumferential Grade III & IV prolapse, we utilize a circular stapling device. It lifts and suspends the prolapsed tissue back inside the rectum—**well above the pain nerve line**—while cutting off excess arterial blood flow simultaneously!

Tailored Proctology

Grades of Hemorrhoids We Treat

Dr. Atul Peters provides tailored minimally invasive procedures based on the severity and prolapse stage of your hemorrhoids.

I

Grade I Piles (Bleeding Without Prolapse)

Internal hemorrhoids that bleed during bowel movements but do not protrude outside the anus. Easily treated with dietary fiber, medical therapy, or outpatient infrared / laser photocoagulation.

II

Grade II Piles (Spontaneous Retraction)

Hemorrhoids that prolapse (pop out) during straining but automatically retract back inside after bowel movements. Treated permanently with **LHP (Laser Hemorrhoidoplasty)** in a 15-minute day-care procedure.

III

Grade III Piles (Manual Repositioning)

Prolapsed hemorrhoids that must be pushed back inside manually with fingers after defecation. Treated using **LHP Laser** or **MIPH Stapled Hemorrhoidopexy** for immediate anatomical restoration.

IV

Grade IV Piles (Permanently Prolapsed)

Severe hemorrhoids that remain permanently prolapsed outside the anus and cannot be pushed back. We combine **MIPH Stapled Surgery** and **LHP Laser** to remove prolapse without external skin cuts.

Ask Dr. Peters
Why is LHP laser and MIPH stapled surgery better than open piles cutting?

Traditional open surgery cuts away pile bundles, leaving raw unstitched wounds in the sensitive anal canal that cause excruciating pain during bowel movements for weeks. **LHP Laser surgery** shrinks blood vessels from inside without external cuts. **MIPH Stapled surgery** lifts prolapsed piles inside the rectum above the pain nerve line. Both methods ensure almost zero pain, no open wounds, and a 2–3 day recovery!

Can piles be cured permanently with creams, tablets, or home remedies?

No. While creams, sitz baths, and high-fiber diets can temporarily relieve itching and mild bleeding in Grade I piles, they cannot reverse stretched, prolapsed blood vessels in Grade II, III, or IV hemorrhoids. Once vascular connective tissue has broken down, minimally invasive laser or stapled intervention is the only definitive medical cure.

Will I have pain or bleeding during bowel movements after surgery?

Because there are no surgical cuts, stitches, or raw open wounds in the sensitive anal skin, postoperative pain and bleeding during bowel movements are dramatically lower than open surgery. We prescribe mild stool softeners and recommend warm sitz baths to ensure bathroom visits remain smooth and comfortable.

What is the risk of hemorrhoids coming back (recurrence)?

When performed by an experienced proctological surgeon like Dr. Atul Peters using advanced radial laser energy or stapled hemorrhoidopexy, the recurrence rate is exceptionally low (< 2–3%). Maintaining a healthy, high-fiber diet and avoiding chronic straining during bowel movements ensures permanent lifelong results.

Is laser and stapled piles surgery covered by health insurance in India?

Yes! Under modern **IRDAI guidelines**, advanced proctological treatments (LHP laser surgery and MIPH stapled hemorrhoidopexy for piles) are medically necessary procedures covered by 100% of health insurance policies, corporate mediclaim networks, TPAs, and CGHS/ECHS schemes in India. Max Smart Hospital Saket provides seamless cashless claim assistance.

When can I resume driving, office work, and gym exercises?

You can walk immediately and return to routine non-strenuous desk work and driving within **2 to 3 days**. For heavy gym lifting, squatting, core exercises, running, or cycling, we advise waiting **2 to 3 weeks** to allow the internal vascular cushions to heal completely.
