Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about visiting our clinic.

Do I need a medical referral to see Dr. Atul Peters?

While a referral from your primary care physician is helpful and recommended, it is not strictly mandatory. You can directly book an appointment for a consultation.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring any recent medical records, blood test reports, details of current medications, and any previous surgical records. This helps Dr. Peters comprehensively evaluate your case.

Do you offer online or video consultations?

Yes, we offer teleconsultations for patients who travel from out of state or internationally. You can request a virtual appointment when contacting our coordinators.

How long does an initial consultation typically take?

Your first consultation usually takes between 30 to 45 minutes. This ensures Dr. Peters has ample time to review your history, discuss your goals, and explain potential treatment options in detail.
