What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measurement that uses your height and weight to categorize whether you're underweight, healthy weight, overweight, or obese for your height.

It is a useful screening tool — not a diagnosis. Talk to your doctor to understand what your BMI means for your overall health.

Formula
Body Weight (kg) Height² (m²) = BMI
Why BMI matters

It helps identify weight-related health risks like type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and high blood pressure.

Limitations of BMI

BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass. Athletes may have a high BMI but low body fat.

Asian BMI Standards

The categories on this page use the Asia-Pacific BMI standards, which are stricter than Western guidelines.

Talk to a Specialist

BMI is just one indicator. A complete health assessment considers diet, exercise, and family history.

Post-Surgery Reference

Phase I — Clear Liquid Diet

A quick reference for the first phase of your post-bariatric diet.

  • Only clear liquids are allowed during this phase.
  • Avoid sweetened beverages — they can cause dumping syndrome.
  • Sip very slowly — small frequent sips throughout the day.
  • Do not use straws — they introduce excess air.
  • Avoid caffeinated, carbonated, and alcoholic beverages.
  • Stop drinking if you feel fullness, pain, or discomfort.
