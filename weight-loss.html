Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first consultation

How much weight will I lose?

Most patients lose 60-80% of their excess weight within 12-18 months after surgery. Exact results vary by procedure:

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy: 60-70% excess weight loss
  • Gastric Bypass: 70-80% excess weight loss
  • Mini Bypass: 75-85% excess weight loss

Weight loss is typically fastest in months 3-6, then stabilizes over 12-18 months.

Is weight loss surgery safe?

Yes — when performed by an experienced bariatric surgeon, these procedures are among the safest major surgeries. The mortality rate is under 0.3%, which is lower than many common procedures like gallbladder removal or hip replacement.

How long is the hospital stay?

Most patients stay 2-3 nights in hospital. Here's the typical timeline:

  • Day 0: Surgery (60-90 minutes)
  • Day 1: Walk with assistance
  • Day 2: Drain removal, discharge
  • Week 1-2: Liquid → Pureed diet
  • Week 2-4: Soft foods
  • Week 4+: Regular foods (small portions)
Can I get pregnant after surgery?

Yes! Many patients who previously struggled with fertility conceive naturally after weight loss. We recommend:

  • Wait 18-24 months after surgery before pregnancy
  • Stable weight is ideal before trying
  • Monthly follow-ups ensure you're ready

Weight loss often improves PCOS and hormonal balance, increasing fertility.

Will I have loose skin?

Some skin excess is normal after major weight loss. Factors affecting skin:

  • Age: Younger = better elasticity
  • Weight lost: More = more loose skin
  • Genetics: Some skin retracts better

We offer skin removal procedures (tummy tuck, arm lift, etc.) once you've reached your goal weight. Typically 12-18 months post-surgery.

What about diet after surgery?

You'll follow a phased diet progression:

  • Weeks 1-2: Clear liquids → Full liquids
  • Weeks 2-4: Pureed/protein shakes
  • Months 1-3: Soft foods
  • Month 3+: Regular foods

Long-term: Smaller portions, more protein, less sugar. Our dietician creates personalized plans.

Is it dolorosaful?

You'll be under general anesthesia during surgery — no pain during the procedure. Post-op discomfort is managed with medication and is typically mild. Most patients describe it as:

  • Mild abdominal soreness (2-3 days)
  • Manageable with prescribed pain relievers
  • Walking same day helps reduce discomfort
How soon can I return to work?

Timeline varies by job type:

  • Sedentary work: 1-2 weeks
  • Office work: 2-3 weeks
  • Physical work: 4-6 weeks

Light walking is encouraged from day 1 to aid recovery and prevent blood clots.
