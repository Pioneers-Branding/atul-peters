Long-Term Success Rules

Avoid the 5 S's

After bariatric surgery, patients are advised to avoid these 5 things. Each plays a critical role in your weight-loss journey and long-term health.

S

Sugar

Avoid to prevent dumping syndrome and support continued weight loss.

S

Spirits

Alcohol adds empty calories and can cause early absorption — strictly avoid.

S

Straw

Drinking through straws introduces excess air — leading to bloating and discomfort.

S

Soda

Carbonated drinks cause abdominal pain, fullness, and bloating.

S

Smoking

Prohibited for respiratory function and surgical recovery.

Phase I · 2-4 Days

Clear Liquid Diet

Begins within 24 hours post-surgery. Liquids provided at the hospital.

  • Only clear liquids are allowed during this phase.
  • Avoid sweetened beverages — they can cause dumping syndrome.
  • Sip very slowly — small frequent sips throughout the day.
  • Do not use straws — they introduce excess air.
  • Avoid caffeinated, carbonated, and alcoholic beverages.
  • Stop drinking if you feel fullness, pain, or discomfort.

Phase II · 2 Weeks

Full Liquid Diet

Begins at hospital discharge. Build on Phase I with more substantial liquids.

  • Stop drinking if you feel fullness, pain or discomfort.
  • Avoid meal skipping — eat at regular intervals.
  • Plan meals in advance to maintain consistency.
  • Drink slowly and consume protein foods first.
  • Start use of a protein supplement as advised.
  • Follow regular scheduled meals with small portions.
  • No straws.
  • Avoid added sugars and high fat foods.
  • Variety! Include different approved foods across the week.
Phase III · 2-4 Weeks

Puree Diet

Progression to soft/pureed foods. A hand blender is recommended.

  • Blended and pureed food is introduced, one new solid food at a time.
  • Pureed meats, pulses, vegetables and fruits form the core.
  • Chew slowly and thoroughly — even pureed foods.
  • Food intolerances vary — track what works for you.
  • Prioritize protein-rich foods at every meal.
  • Gradually increase meal size to 150 - 200ml per sitting.
  • Avoid meal skipping — maintain a regular schedule.

Phase IV · Post 4 Weeks · Long Term

Soft to Normal Diet

Gradual increment from soft diet to a normal, sustainable eating pattern.

A bariatric soft diet provides foods whose texture is modified — a comfortable step back to a normal, sustainable eating pattern.

Eating Behaviors

  • Eat only at regular intervals — three small meals + two small snacks.
  • Chew well and eat slowly — chew to liquid consistency.
  • Eat your proteins first at each meal.
  • Avoid drinking liquids 30 minutes before and after meals.
  • Small portion size — major on portions to avoid overeating.
  • Listen to your body — different capacities and appetites.
The 8-Step Process

Your Step-by-Step Journey

From your first phone call through long-term nutrition follow-up — every step of the way, our team is with you.

01
First Contact

Meet the Program Manager

Your first point of contact. The program manager will help you answer all your initial queries and doubts regarding bariatric and metabolic surgery.

02
Assessment

Meeting with Bariatric Dietician

  • Personal details
  • Body composition analysis
  • Medical history (family & personal)
  • Detailed explanation of diet post-surgery
03
Consultation

Visit with the Surgeon

  • Brief physical examination
  • Detailed understanding of the procedure
  • Suggested preoperative evaluations
04
Evaluations

The Next Step

  • Psychological evaluation
  • Blood test, chest X-ray, Echo, ECG, UGIE, PFT & ultrasound
  • Cardiologist, Endocrinologist, Pulmonologist, Intensivist, Internist
  • PAC and Obesity Board recommendation
05
Preparation

Preparations for Surgery

  • Follow liquid diet 1 day before surgery
  • No alcohol or smoking one day prior
  • Bring CPAP machine if used
  • Take thyroid, BP, heart, asthma medication with sip of water
  • Take a nice shower before coming to hospital
06
Surgery Day

Day of the Surgery

  • Report at reception at given time
  • Settle in room, consent taken, procedure explained
  • Shifted to pre-op area
  • Operation theatre, then post-op recovery, then ICU
07
Recovery

Next Day of Surgery

  • Shifted to the room
  • Discharged the same day, if fit
08
Lifetime

Nutrition Post Bariatric Surgery

Diet follow-up for life. We ensure:

  • Sufficient energy and nutrients to support tissue healing and preserve lean body mass
  • Foods & beverages that decrease reflux, early satiety, dumping syndrome
  • Maximized weight loss and long-term weight maintenance
