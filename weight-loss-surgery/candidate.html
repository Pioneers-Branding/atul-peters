Associated Conditions

Obesity-Related Diseases

If you suffer from any of these conditions along with excess weight, surgery can be life-changing — and often life-saving.

Type 2 Diabetes

Strongest indication for bariatric surgery. ~85% remission rates, often within days.

Hypertension

High blood pressure normalizes in majority of patients as weight decreases.

Sleep Apnea

Resolution of obstructive sleep apnea in most cases — many come off CPAP.

Joint Disease

Chronic joint pain and arthritis dramatically improve with weight loss.

Dyslipidemia

Elevated cholesterol and triglycerides normalize post-surgery.

Heart Disease

Cardiovascular risk factors reduce significantly with surgical weight loss.

PCOS / Infertility

Polycystic ovarian syndrome and fertility outcomes improve dramatically.

Fatty Liver

Reversal of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) common post-surgery.

Important Considerations

When Surgery May Not Be Right

While bariatric surgery is safe and effective for most candidates, certain conditions require additional evaluation or may delay surgery. Dr. Peters' team conducts a thorough pre-op assessment to ensure the best outcomes.

Untreated psychiatric conditions Active substance abuse, uncontrolled depression, or eating disorders require stabilization before surgery.
Inability to commit to lifestyle change Surgery is a tool — long-term success requires adherence to diet, exercise, and follow-up care.
Severe medical instability Recent heart attack, active cancer treatment, or severe organ failure require optimization before elective surgery.
Pregnancy or planned pregnancy Women should avoid pregnancy for 12-18 months post-surgery. Surgery is recommended after completing family.

FAQ

Eligibility Questions

Common questions about who qualifies for weight loss surgery — answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

Still unsure?
What if my BMI is below 35 — am I still eligible?

For Asian patients, the threshold may be lowered to BMI ≥ 32.5 per IFSO-Asia guidelines. Additionally, if you have uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes, you may qualify for metabolic surgery at BMI 27.5-32.5. A consultation will confirm your eligibility.

Can I have surgery if I have Type 2 Diabetes?

Yes — Type 2 Diabetes is the single strongest indication for bariatric surgery. Studies show ~85% remission rates, often within days of surgery, independent of weight loss due to changes in gut hormones GLP-1, PYY and ghrelin.

I'm 65 years old. Is it too late for surgery?

Not necessarily. We routinely operate on patients up to age 70 after cardiac and pulmonary clearance. Each case is evaluated individually. Older patients often see dramatic improvements in mobility, diabetes, and quality of life.

What if I've already had weight loss surgery?

Revision bariatric surgery is an option for patients who experienced weight regain after sleeve or band surgery. Roux-en-Y is the standard revision option. Dr. Peters has extensive experience with complex revisions.

How long is the waiting period from consultation to surgery?

Typically 6-8 weeks. This includes comprehensive pre-op evaluation (physician, dietitian, psychiatrist, pulmonologist, cardiologist) and a 2-week liver-shrinking diet before surgery.

Does insurance cover the surgery?

Most IRDA-approved bariatric covers and cashless networks (Max, MediAssist, MDIndia, Paramount) reimburse the procedure when BMI and comorbidity criteria are met. EMI and 0% financing options are also available for self-pay patients.
