Excellence in Minimal Access & GI Surgery

Modern gastrointestinal and metabolic surgery has been revolutionized by minimal access technology. Instead of traditional open abdominal incisions that cause substantial muscle trauma and prolonged recovery, Dr. Atul Peters and his team utilize high-definition 4K laparoscopy and the surgical precision of the da Vinci Robotic System.

While Dr. Peters is celebrated globally for his bariatric and metabolic achievements, his surgical department at Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital, Saket is a premier tertiary referral center for complex gastrointestinal disorders, abdominal wall reconstructions, and revision operations. When patients present with recurrent hernias, biliary disease, severe reflux, or complications from older bariatric surgeries performed elsewhere, our team provides state-of-the-art surgical remediation.

Minimal access surgery operates through 3 to 4 tiny keyhole incisions (5 to 12 mm). By inserting specialized optical cameras and micro-articulating instruments, Dr. Peters can visualize vital abdominal structures with 10x magnification. In complex reconstructive procedures—such as repairing massive incisional hernias after prior open surgery or converting an eroded Lap-Band into a Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass—this sub-millimeter precision ensures minimal blood loss, preservation of delicate nerve pathways, and virtually zero wound infections.

Whether you require a routine outpatient gallbladder removal, advanced robotic hernia repair, or specialized metabolic surgery for diabetes, our multidisciplinary team combines three decades of surgical expertise with compassionate, structured perioperative care—getting you back to active, healthy living within days.

Specialized Procedures

Comprehensive Minimal Access Care: Key Focus Areas

From correcting older bariatric surgeries to reconstructing complex abdominal wall hernias and resolving biliary disorders, Dr. Atul Peters brings world-class precision to every operation.

Revision & Conversion Bariatric Surgery

Weight regain, stomach dilation, or band complications can occur years after an initial bariatric procedure. As a published researcher on surgical conversions, Dr. Peters specializes in safely converting failed Lap-Bands or Sleeve Gastrectomies into Roux-en-Y Bypass or Mini Gastric Bypass—restoring metabolic momentum and resolving acid reflux.

Complex Abdominal Wall & Hernia Repair

Hernias occur when internal organs push through a weakness in the abdominal muscle wall. Dr. Peters performs advanced laparoscopic and robotic hernia repairs (Ventral, Umbilical, Incisional, and Inguinal) using state-of-the-art eTEP and TAR mesh reconstruction techniques—strengthening the abdominal core without tension.

Laparoscopic Gallbladder Surgery (Cholecystectomy)

Gallstones are a frequent cause of severe abdominal pain, nausea, and biliary inflammation. Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the gold-standard treatment, removing the diseased gallbladder through 3 tiny keyhole incisions. When indicated, this procedure can be performed concomitantly during bariatric surgery.

Anti-Reflux & Hiatal Hernia Surgery

Severe Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) and large hiatal hernias can cause debilitating heartburn, regurgitation, and esophageal damage. Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication repairs the diaphragmatic opening and wraps the upper stomach around the lower esophagus, creating a natural valve that stops acid reflux permanently.

Recovery & Surgical Guidelines

Our structured enhanced recovery protocol ensures a smooth, rapid return to your active daily life.

✓ Do's After GI & Laparoscopic Surgery

  • Start walking within 4 to 6 hours after surgery to prevent blood clots
  • Keep your keyhole waterproof dressings clean and dry for the first 3 days
  • Drink plenty of water and warm fluids to maintain hydration
  • Follow the specific diet progression prescribed for your operation
  • Attend your scheduled 1-week follow-up visit for dressing review

✗ Don'ts After GI & Laparoscopic Surgery

  • Never lift heavy objects (> 5 kg) for 3 to 4 weeks after hernia repair
  • Do not ignore signs of fever, severe increasing pain, or redness at incision sites
  • Avoid smoking or tobacco use (impairs tissue healing and increases ulcer risk)
  • Avoid straining during bowel movements (take prescribed stool softeners)
  • Do not drive until you are completely free of pain and pain medications

Frequently Asked

GI & Minimal Access Questions Answered

10 of the most common clinical and practical questions about our advanced laparoscopic, GI, and robotic procedures, answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

What types of surgeries are included under Advanced Laparoscopic and GI Procedures?

As Vice Chairman of the Department of Bariatric, Minimal Access & Robotic Surgery at Max Smart Hospital, Dr. Atul Peters performs a comprehensive range of GI and metabolic operations. These include Revision Bariatric Surgery, complex abdominal wall and inguinal Hernia Repairs (using eTEP and TAR techniques), Laparoscopic Gallbladder Removal (Cholecystectomy), Anti-Reflux GERD Surgery (Nissen Fundoplication), and specialized metabolic procedures for diabetes.

Why is robotic surgery (da Vinci) beneficial for complex hernia and GI surgeries?

The da Vinci Robotic Surgical System offers 10x 3D high-definition magnification and wristed instruments that rotate 360 degrees—far beyond human hand capability. In complex procedures like large abdominal wall hernia repair or difficult bariatric revisions, robotics allows sub-millimeter suturing in confined spaces, minimizing tissue trauma, blood loss, and post-operative pain.

What is Revision Bariatric Surgery, and when is it recommended?

Revision bariatric surgery is performed to correct or modify a previous bariatric operation. It is recommended for patients who have experienced significant weight regain, band slippage or erosion from older Lap-Bands, stomach pouch dilation after Sleeve Gastrectomy, or severe intractable acid reflux. Dr. Peters is a renowned authority in safely converting failed sleeves or bands to Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass or Mini Gastric Bypass.

How is laparoscopic gallbladder removal (cholecystectomy) performed?

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the gold-standard treatment for gallstones and gallbladder infection. Using 3 to 4 tiny keyhole incisions, the surgeon detaches and removes the diseased gallbladder. Because gallstones are common during obesity and rapid weight loss, Dr. Peters frequently performs gallbladder surgery safely and efficiently as a standalone procedure or concomitantly during bariatric surgery.

Can an incisional or abdominal wall hernia occur after bariatric surgery or weight loss?

Yes. While rare after modern laparoscopic surgery, patients who previously underwent older open surgeries or who have lost massive amounts of weight may develop abdominal wall defects or incisional hernias. Dr. Peters utilizes advanced minimally invasive abdominal wall reconstruction techniques (such as eTEP and TAR mesh placement) to permanently restore abdominal core strength.

What is laparoscopic anti-reflux surgery (Nissen Fundoplication) for GERD?

For patients suffering from severe chronic Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) or large hiatal hernias that do not respond to antacid medications, laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication provides a permanent cure. The surgeon repairs the diaphragmatic hernia defect and wraps the upper portion of the stomach around the lower esophagus to reinforce the valve and prevent acid reflux.

How long is the hospital stay for laparoscopic hernia or gallbladder surgery?

Because of minimal access keyhole incisions and nerve-sparing techniques, recovery is exceptionally fast. Most patients undergoing laparoscopic gallbladder removal or standard hernia repair are discharged within 24 to 48 hours and can resume normal non-strenuous daily activities within 3 to 5 days.

Can gallbladder removal and bariatric surgery or hernia repair be performed at the same time?

Yes. When clinically indicated and carefully evaluated during pre-operative workup, concomitant procedures—such as performing a Sleeve Gastrectomy and removing a symptomatic stone-filled gallbladder, or repairing an umbilical hernia during bariatric surgery—can be safely combined under a single anesthesia exposure.

What is metabolic surgery for non-obese or low-BMI patients with Type 2 Diabetes?

While standard bariatric bypass is performed for BMI ≥ 35 (or ≥ 32.5 with diabetes), specialized metabolic procedures like Ileal Interposition or Proximal Jejunal Bypass are tailored specifically to reverse uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes in lower-BMI individuals (BMI 27.5 to 32.5 kg/m²). These procedures alter intestinal hormonal signaling to stimulate insulin secretion without causing excessive weight loss.

Are GI, hernia, and revision surgeries covered by medical insurance in India?

Yes. Gallbladder surgery (cholecystectomy), hernia repairs, anti-reflux surgery, and medically indicated revision bariatric surgeries are recognized medical treatments covered under standard IRDA-approved health insurance policies in India.
