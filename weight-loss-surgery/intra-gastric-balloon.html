Procedure Overview

What is the Intra-Gastric Balloon?

The Intra-Gastric Balloon (IGB) is a temporary, non-surgical tool designed to kickstart weight loss by creating a feeling of early satiety. By occupying approximately 30% of the stomach's volume, it helps patients eat smaller portions without feeling hungry, enabling a structured transition to healthier eating habits.

Unlike traditional bariatric procedures such as a Sleeve Gastrectomy or Gastric Bypass, the IGB requires no surgical incisions and no anatomical alterations to the stomach or intestines. The soft, silicone balloon is inserted endoscopically through the mouth while you are under mild sedation, and then filled with 500-700ml of sterile saline solution. The entire process takes just 15-20 minutes, allowing you to go home the same day.

The balloon is typically left in place for 6 to 12 months (depending on the specific model used) and serves as a powerful catalyst for weight loss. During this period, you will participate in a comprehensive support program, working closely with our dietitians to reform your eating patterns. After the balloon is removed, these newly established habits are key to maintaining your weight loss long-term.

Balloon Options

Types of Intra-Gastric Balloons

We offer multiple FDA-approved balloon systems to suit different weight loss goals and patient preferences. Dr. Peters will help you select the ideal model during your consultation.

Orbera® Balloon

The global leader in gastric balloons. It is placed endoscopically and filled with saline. Typically left in place for 6 months and has a long track record of safety and proven efficacy.

Elipse / Allurion Swallowable

The world's first procedure-less balloon. You simply swallow a capsule containing the deflated balloon. It is filled via a thin catheter, and after 4 months, it naturally deflates and passes safely.

Spatz3® Adjustable Balloon

A highly versatile balloon that can remain in the stomach for up to 12 months. It features a unique adjustable port, allowing Dr. Peters to increase or decrease the volume to manage intolerance or boost weight loss.

Step-by-Step Process

How the IGB Works

A simple, outpatient procedure — no surgery, no hospital stay, no scars.

01

Consultation & Eligibility

Meet with Dr. Peters for a thorough evaluation including BMI assessment, endoscopy, and discussion of your weight loss goals to confirm you're a good candidate.

02

Endoscopic Placement

Under mild sedation, the deflated balloon is inserted through your mouth into the stomach via an endoscope. No incisions needed — takes 15-20 minutes.

03

Filled & Activated

The balloon is filled with 500-700ml of sterile saline. It occupies ~30% of stomach volume, creating early satiety.

04

6-Month Journey

Over 6 months, you follow a structured diet plan with regular follow-ups. Patients typically lose 10-15% of total body weight.

05

Removal & Beyond

After 6 months, the balloon is removed endoscopically. You transition to a maintenance program to keep the weight off long-term.

Nutrition & Lifestyle

Living With the Intra-Gastric Balloon

The balloon is a tool, not a cure. Your success depends on embracing a structured eating plan and healthy lifestyle during the 6-12 months it remains in your stomach.

✓ Do's

  • Eat slowly and chew food thoroughly
  • Stop eating as soon as you feel full
  • Drink plenty of water between meals (at least 2L daily)
  • Prioritize protein at every meal
  • Exercise regularly (wait 2-3 weeks post-placement)

✗ Don'ts

  • Do not drink liquids 30 mins before or after meals
  • Avoid high-calorie liquids (soda, milkshakes, alcohol)
  • Do not lie down immediately after eating (prevents reflux)
  • Avoid sticky or tough foods that are hard to digest
  • Do not eat large portions (this causes cramping)

Common Questions

IGB FAQs

01
Is the IGB procedure painful?

No — the procedure is done under mild sedation. Most patients report minimal discomfort during placement. However, it is common to experience nausea, cramping, or vomiting for the first 1-3 days after placement as your stomach adjusts to the balloon. This is managed proactively with medication.

02
How much weight can I expect to lose?

On average, patients lose 10-15% of their total body weight over 6 months. Some lose more, especially when strictly adhering to the prescribed diet and lifestyle changes. The balloon is a tool to help you control portions, but long-term success relies entirely on your commitment to habit change.

03
What happens if the balloon leaks?

Modern IGBs are extremely durable and contain blue dye in the saline. In the rare event that the balloon leaks, you'll immediately notice blue-tinged urine. This acts as a reliable warning signal to contact our team, and the deflated balloon is safely removed endoscopically.

04
Will I regain weight after removal?

Because the stomach is not permanently altered, weight maintenance relies entirely on the new eating habits you developed during the 6-month period. Patients who continue to follow their maintenance plan and exercise routine are highly successful at keeping the weight off long-term.
