Understanding Obesity

What is Weight Loss Surgery?

Obesity is a major health hazard with worldwide rates having tripled since 1975. In 2016, more than 1.9 billion adults aged 18+ were overweight, and over 650 million were obese with related health complications.

India's Obesity Crisis

The number of obese people has doubled in the last 10 years (NFHS), with urban populations notably more affected than rural counterparts.

Complex Medical Condition

Accounts for 80-85% of Type-2 Diabetes risk, runs in families, and requires long-term lifestyle changes beyond simple dieting.

Childhood Epidemic

41 million children under 5 are overweight or obese — creating intergenerational health challenges that demand early intervention.

Root Causes

What Causes Obesity?

The basic cause is an energy imbalance between calories consumed and calories spent — but the underlying factors are often more complex.

01

Energy-Dense Foods

Excessive intake of high-fat, high-sugar, processed foods that are easy to overconsume without feeling full.

02

Sedentary Lifestyle

Decrease in physical activity due to desk jobs, motorized transport, and increasing urbanization.

03

Genetics & Family

Obesity tends to run in families, with shared diet, lifestyle and genetic factors amplifying the risk.

04

Environment & Behavior

Stress levels, sleep patterns, emotional triggers, and environmental cues all play a significant role.

Health Impact

Common Health Consequences

Obesity isn't just a cosmetic concern — it's a medical problem that increases your risk of other serious diseases.

Cardiovascular

Increased risk for heart disease and stroke due to elevated blood pressure and cholesterol.

2-3x higher risk

Type 2 Diabetes

Severe impact on insulin regulation, with obesity being the leading modifiable risk factor.

6x higher risk

Musculoskeletal

Degenerative joint disease and chronic pain, especially in knees, hips, and lower back.

4-5x higher risk

Certain Cancers

Increased risk for breast, colon, endometrial, liver, kidney, and pancreatic cancers.

1.5-2x higher risk

Childhood obesity is associated with a higher chance of obesity, premature death, and disability in adulthood — making early intervention critical.

Low-calorie, low-fat diets paired with exercise are typically the first line of treatment. Crash diets and appetite suppressants are only appropriate under very specific conditions and strict medical supervision.

Definitions

Definitions of Obesity

Body Mass Index (BMI) is the standard medical screening tool used to categorize weight status. Asian populations use lower thresholds than Western standards.

BMI (Body Mass Index) = Body Weight (kg) ÷ Height2 (m)

FAQ

Common Questions

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about weight loss surgery, recovery, and long-term outcomes.

Still have questions?
Am I a candidate for weight loss surgery?

You may be a candidate if your BMI is ≥ 35 kg/m² (with or without comorbidities) or ≥ 32.5 kg/m² with Type 2 Diabetes. For Asian patients, thresholds may be lowered by 2.5 points per IFSO-Asia guidelines. A consultation with Dr. Peters will confirm eligibility.

How much weight will I lose after surgery?

Most patients lose 60-80% of excess body weight within 9-12 months. Sleeve gastrectomy averages ~65%, Roux-en-Y ~80%, and Mini Gastric Bypass ~75%. Individual results vary based on adherence to diet and exercise guidelines.

Is bariatric surgery safe?

Modern bariatric surgery has a complication rate of less than 2% in experienced hands. At Max Hospital, Dr. Peters' team performs over 1,500 bariatric procedures annually with outcomes matching international Centers of Excellence benchmarks.

Will my insurance cover the surgery?

Most IRDA-approved bariatric covers and cashless networks (Max, MediAssist, MDIndia, Paramount) reimburse the procedure when BMI and comorbidity criteria are met. EMI and 0% financing options are also available for self-pay patients.

How long is the recovery period?

Most patients stay 1-2 nights at Max Smart Hospital, Saket. Return to desk work is typically within 1-2 weeks. Full recovery, including return to strenuous activity, is usually within 4-6 weeks for laparoscopic and robotic procedures.

Will I regain weight after surgery?

Most patients maintain 70-80% of their excess weight loss at 10 years. Some weight regain of 5-10% can occur around 18-24 months if dietary and exercise guidelines are not followed. Long-term success depends on adherence to follow-up, protein intake and physical activity.
