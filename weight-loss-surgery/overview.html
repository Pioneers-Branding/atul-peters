Understanding Obesity

What is Weight Loss Surgery?

Obesity is a major health hazard with worldwide rates having tripled since 1975. In 2016, more than 1.9 billion adults aged 18+ were overweight, and over 650 million were obese with related health complications.

In India, the number of obese people has doubled in the last 10 years, according to the National Family Health Survey. Urban populations are notably more prone to obesity than their rural counterparts.

Research confirms that obesity is difficult to treat and often requires long-term lifestyle changes. It accounts for 80-85% of the risk of developing Type-2 Diabetes and tends to run in families — suggesting both genetic and environmental contributions.

Childhood obesity is also rising, with 41 million children under 5 estimated to be overweight or obese in 2016 — affecting the newborns of these adults and creating intergenerational health challenges.

Root Causes

What Causes Obesity?

The basic cause is an energy imbalance between calories consumed and calories spent.

  • Excessive intake of energy-dense foods — high in fat, sugar, and processed ingredients that are easy to overconsume.
  • Decrease in physical activity — due to sedentary work, changing modes of transportation, and increasing urbanization.
  • Genetic and family factors — obesity tends to run in families, with shared diet and lifestyle habits amplifying the risk.
  • Environmental and behavioral factors — what and how often a person eats, stress levels, sleep patterns, and emotional triggers all play a role.
Health Impact

Common Health Consequences

Obesity isn't just a cosmetic concern — it's a medical problem that increases your risk of other serious diseases.

Cardiovascular

Increased risk for heart disease and stroke due to elevated blood pressure and cholesterol.

Type 2 Diabetes

Severe impact on insulin regulation, with obesity being the leading modifiable risk factor.

Musculoskeletal

Degenerative joint disease and chronic pain, especially in knees, hips, and lower back.

Certain Cancers

Increased risk for breast, colon, endometrial, liver, kidney, and pancreatic cancers.

Childhood obesity is associated with a higher chance of obesity, premature death, and disability in adulthood — making early intervention critical.

Low-calorie, low-fat diets paired with exercise are typically the first line of treatment. Crash diets and appetite suppressants are only appropriate under very specific conditions and strict medical supervision.
