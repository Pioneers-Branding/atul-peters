Procedure Overview

What are Banded Bariatric Procedures?

Banded Bariatric Surgery is an evolutionary advancement in weight loss surgery designed to solve the single greatest long-term challenge in bariatrics: pouch dilation and weight regain. By placing an inert, medical-grade silicone ring around the stomach pouch or sleeve, banded procedures combine the metabolic power of modern bariatric surgery with a permanent, non-stretchable mechanical safeguard.

In standard bariatric operations such as Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (RYGB) or Sleeve Gastrectomy (LSG), the stomach tissue is living, elastic muscle. Over a period of 5, 10, or 15 years—especially if a patient struggles with portion control, stress grazing, or drinking carbonated beverages—the stomach pouch or gastric sleeve can gradually stretch and dilate. As the pouch capacity increases and the stoma outlet widens, food empties into the small intestine too rapidly. Consequently, patients lose their sense of early satiety, begin consuming larger portions, and may experience significant weight regain.

In a Banded Bariatric Procedure (such as a Banded Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass or Banded Sleeve Gastrectomy), Dr. Atul Peters places a calibrated, biocompatible silicone ring (such as the Fobi Ring or GaBP Ring) around the upper stomach pouch approximately 2 cm above the intestinal connection or mid-sleeve. Because medical-grade silicone is completely inert and cannot stretch, it maintains a constant, calibrated 2.0 to 2.5 cm outlet for life. This gentle restriction delays gastric emptying, ensuring that even a small, healthy meal creates a profound and lasting feeling of fullness that endures for decades.

As one of the few bariatric surgeons in Asia with deep, multi-decade expertise in banded primary and revision procedures—and author of landmark published research on hand-sewn Fobi Ring bypass techniques in Obesity Surgery—Dr. Peters performs banded surgeries laparoscopically and with robotic da Vinci precision at Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital, Saket.

Banded Surgery in Delhi: Complete Clinical Guide

Banded Bariatric Procedures represent the pinnacle of long-term weight loss security. By combining the metabolic hormone reprogramming of gastric bypass or sleeve gastrectomy with a permanent silicone ring reinforcement, Dr. Atul Peters offers patients at Max Smart Hospital a surgical solution engineered to resist weight regain for life.

Why Add a Silicone Ring or Band?

Stomach tissue naturally relaxes and adapts over time. Without reinforcement, up to 20% of bariatric patients experience some pouch dilation and weight regain over 10 years. The medical-grade silicone ring acts as an exogenous, non-stretchable barrier that prevents dilation, ensuring that your portion control and early satiety remain just as powerful in year 15 as in year 1.

Dr. Peters' Landmark Research & Technique

In 2014, Dr. Atul Peters authored a peer-reviewed clinical study in Obesity Surgery titled "Hand Sewn Banded Bypass Using Fobi's Ring." His specialized seromuscular suturing technique anchors the ring securely into the gastric wall, virtually eliminating historical risks of ring slippage or erosion (< 1% incidence).

Banded Bypass vs. Banded Sleeve

Banded Bypass (BRYGB): Integrates the ring just above the gastrojejunal anastomosis, pairing high diabetes remission (~85%) with permanent restriction. Banded Sleeve (BLSG): Places the ring around the mid-sleeve, preventing sleeve dilation in patients with high BMI or severe grazing habits without rerouting the intestines.

Superior 10 to 20-Year Clinical Data

Published long-term bariatric literature confirms that patients who receive a banded procedure maintain an average of 15% to 20% greater excess weight loss at 10-year follow-up compared to non-banded cohorts, with significantly higher rates of long-term comorbidity remission and patient satisfaction.

The Procedure

How Banded Surgery Works

A step-by-step walkthrough of Dr. Atul Peters' precision surgical technique

01

Pre-Surgery Evaluation & Optimization

A thorough multidisciplinary evaluation including body composition analysis, nutritional counseling, physician review, pulmonologist and cardiologist clearance, and psychiatric screening. Thromboembolism prophylaxis is initiated prior to surgery.

02

Positioning & Minimal Access Port Placement

Under general anesthesia, 3 to 4 tiny incisions (5–12 mm) are made in the abdomen to accommodate laparoscopic camera and precision instruments. When performed robotically with da Vinci, 3D high-definition vision and tremor-free articulating arms allow unmatched surgical precision.

03

Creating the Primary Pouch or Sleeve

For a Banded Bypass, a 30–40 ml egg-sized gastric pouch is created using surgical staplers. For a Banded Sleeve, ~75% of the outer stomach is removed over a calibrated sizing bougie to form a narrow vertical sleeve.

04

Ring Calibration & Placement

A sterile, medical-grade silicone ring (such as a Fobi Ring or GaBP Ring) is positioned around the gastric pouch approximately 2 cm above the intestinal anastomosis (in bypass) or mid-stomach (in sleeve). It creates a calibrated 2.0 to 2.5 cm non-stretchable outlet.

05

Hand-Sewn Fixation & Anti-Slippage Suturing

Using Dr. Peters' specialized technique, non-absorbable seromuscular fixation sutures are placed over the ring, anchoring it into the gastric tissue. This critical step prevents ring migration, slippage, or erosion over decades of use.

06

Anastomosis & Intraoperative Leak Testing

The gastrointestinal routing is completed. An intraoperative leak test (using methylene blue dye or air) verifies complete seal integrity. Incisions are closed cleanly with medical glue without external skin sutures.

Post-Surgery Diet

Eating with a Silicone Ring

Because of the calibrated 2.0–2.5 cm ring outlet, mindful eating and thorough chewing become your greatest allies.

✓ Do's for Banded Patients

  • Chew every bite of solid food 20 to 30 times until liquid
  • Take small bites using a baby fork or teaspoon
  • Pause 1 to 2 minutes between bites to allow food to pass the ring
  • Aim for 60 to 80g of high-quality protein daily
  • Stop eating immediately at the very first sensation of fullness

✗ Don'ts for Banded Patients

  • Never swallow large, unchewed chunks of meat or bread (can block ring)
  • Do not drink liquids while eating solid meals (wait 30 min before/after)
  • Avoid carbonated and fizz drinks (can cause uncomfortable pressure)
  • Avoid tough, fibrous skins or stringy vegetables without pureeing
  • Never use straws or gulp liquids quickly

Frequently Asked

Banded Surgery Questions Answered

10 of the most common clinical and practical questions about Banded Bariatric Procedures, answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

What is the difference between standard gastric bypass and banded gastric bypass?

In a standard Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass, the stomach pouch is made of living muscle that can gradually dilate over 5 to 10 years, which may allow larger meal portions and lead to weight regain. In a Banded Gastric Bypass, a biocompatible medical-grade silicone ring is placed around the lower end of the stomach pouch. This ring acts as a permanent, non-stretchable outlet that maintains prolonged satiety and prevents pouch stretching over decades.

What is a Fobi Ring or GaBP Ring made of, and is it safe inside the body?

The ring is made of inert, medical-grade silicone or radiopaque Silastic material specifically engineered for permanent implantation in the human body. It does not trigger immune rejection, degrade over time, or interfere with MRI scans, X-rays, or airport security detectors.

Can the silicone ring slip, erode, or cause obstruction?

With historical older techniques, ring slippage was occasionally reported. However, Dr. Atul Peters utilizes a precision hand-sewn seromuscular fixation technique (published in his landmark 2014 study in Obesity Surgery), where non-absorbable sutures securely anchor the ring into the stomach wall. This modern technique has reduced slippage and erosion rates to less than 1%.

Does a banded procedure make eating solid foods more difficult?

Because the ring creates a calibrated 2.0 to 2.5 cm outlet, patients must adopt disciplined eating habits: taking small bites, chewing each mouthful 20 to 30 times, and eating slowly. If food is swallowed in large, unchewed chunks, it can temporarily sit above the ring and cause discomfort or regurgitation. Once proper chewing habits become second nature, patients enjoy a wide variety of healthy solid foods comfortably.

Why is Dr. Atul Peters considered a pioneer in banded bariatric procedures?

Dr. Peters is among the few surgeons in Asia with extensive clinical experience in both primary and revision banded bariatric procedures. He authored peer-reviewed research on Hand Sewn Banded Bypass Using Fobi's Ring in Obesity Surgery (2014) and routinely performs complex robotic and laparoscopic banded surgeries at Max Smart Hospital with exceptional safety and long-term success rates.

Am I a better candidate for Banded Bypass or Banded Sleeve Gastrectomy?

Banded Bypass is ideal for patients with severe Type 2 Diabetes, metabolic syndrome, or pre-existing GERD/reflux who also want long-term protection against weight regain. Banded Sleeve Gastrectomy is excellent for patients with high BMI or grazing habits who do not have reflux and prefer not to bypass the intestinal tract. Dr. Peters will evaluate your clinical profile to recommend the best option.

Can a banded procedure be performed robotically with da Vinci?

Yes. Dr. Peters frequently performs Banded Bypass and Banded Sleeve using the da Vinci robotic surgical system. The robot provides 10x 3D high-definition visualization and 360-degree articulating instruments, allowing sub-millimeter precision when suturing and anchoring the silicone ring around the stomach pouch.

What happens if I experience weight regain years after my initial bariatric surgery? Can a band be added?

Yes. Adding a silicone ring or band is one of the most effective and common revision bariatric procedures for patients who have experienced pouch dilation and weight regain after a primary Sleeve Gastrectomy or standard Gastric Bypass. It restores restriction without requiring complex re-stapling of the stomach.

How much weight can I expect to lose and maintain over 10 years with a banded procedure?

Clinical studies demonstrate that patients undergoing banded bariatric procedures achieve an average of 80% to 85% excess weight loss (EWL). More importantly, long-term 10-to-15-year follow-up data shows that banded cohorts maintain 15% to 20% higher weight loss durability compared to non-banded cohorts.

Is banded bariatric surgery covered by health insurance in India?

Yes. Like standard bariatric procedures, banded surgeries are covered under IRDA-approved medical insurance policies in India when standard medical criteria are met (BMI ≥ 35, or BMI ≥ 32.5 with obesity-related comorbidities like Type 2 Diabetes or sleep apnea). Cashless facility is available across major TPAs and insurance networks at Max Smart Hospital.
