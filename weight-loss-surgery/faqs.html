Weight Loss How much weight will I lose?

Most patients lose 60-80% of their excess weight within 12-18 months after surgery. Exact results vary by procedure:

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy: 60-70% excess weight loss
  • Gastric Bypass: 70-80% excess weight loss
  • Mini Bypass: 75-85% excess weight loss

Weight loss is typically fastest in months 3-6, then stabilizes over 12-18 months.

02
Weight Loss When will I see results?

Most patients see immediate weight loss within the first week:

  • Week 1: 3-7kg (water weight)
  • Month 1: 10-15% of excess weight
  • 3 months: 25-30% of excess weight
  • 6 months: 50-60% of excess weight
  • 12 months: 70-80% of excess weight

The timeline varies based on your starting weight, procedure type, and adherence to dietary guidelines.

03
Weight Loss Will I regain weight?

Weight regain is rare when patients follow post-op guidelines. At our center:

  • 95% of patients maintain long-term weight loss (5+ years)
  • Only 2-3% regain more than 10% of lost weight

Key factors preventing regain: regular follow-ups, exercise, and nutritional support.

04
Weight Loss Will I lose hair?

Temporary hair loss can occur 3-6 months after surgery due to rapid weight loss:

  • Affects 20-30% of patients (mild to moderate)
  • Usually starts at 3 months, peaks at 4-5 months
  • Regrowth typically begins at 6-9 months
  • 100% recovery by 12 months for most

We supplement protein and biotin to minimize this. Hair usually returns fuller and healthier.

05
Weight Loss Will I have loose skin?

Loose skin depends on age, genetics, and weight loss rate:

  • Young patients (<30): Minimal loose skin
  • Older patients (40+): More likely to need plastic surgery
  • BMI >50: Often need body contouring

Exercise, hydration, and protein help. Plastic surgery is available for significant excess skin.

Weight Loss How will my cholesterol change?

Dramatic improvements in cholesterol occur after surgery:

  • Total cholesterol: Decrease 30-40%
  • LDL ("bad"): Decrease 40-50%
  • HDL ("good"): Increase 10-20%
  • Triglycerides: Decrease 50-70%

Many patients can stop cholesterol medications within 6-12 months.

Safety & Risk What are the common complications?

Major complications are very rare at high-volume centers:

  • Leak: 0.5-2% (highest risk in first 30 days)
  • Stricture: 1-3% (scar tissue narrowing)
  • Infection: 1-2%
  • Pulmonary embolism: 0.1-0.3%

Our protocols include blood thinners, early ambulation, and antibiotics to prevent complications.

11
Safety & Risk What if I have complications?

Our team is prepared for any situation with immediate response protocols:

  • 24/7 on-call surgeons for emergencies
  • Quick intervention protocols established
  • Complication management with 95% success rate

Surgery is only needed in less than 2% of cases for leaks or bleeding.

Recovery How long is the hospital stay?

Most patients stay 2-3 nights in hospital. Here's the typical timeline:

  • Day 0: Surgery (60-90 minutes)
  • Day 1: Walk with assistance
  • Day 2: Drain removal, discharge
  • Week 1-2: Liquid → Pureed diet
  • Week 2-4: Soft foods
  • Week 4+: Regular foods (small portions)

Lifestyle Can I get pregnant after surgery?

Yes! Many patients who previously struggled with fertility conceive naturally after weight loss. We recommend:

  • Wait 18-24 months after surgery before pregnancy
  • Stable weight is ideal before trying
  • Monthly follow-ups ensure you're ready

Pregnancy after bariatric surgery is generally safer than pregnancy with obesity.

17
Lifestyle Can I exercise after surgery?

Yes! Exercise is encouraged and essential for long-term success:

  • Week 1-2: Short walks (15-20 minutes)
  • Week 3-4: Increase to 30-40 minutes
  • Month 2+: Add strength training
  • 3 months: Resume moderate cardio
  • 6 months: Return to normal exercise routine

Exercise enhances weight loss, maintains muscle mass, and improves mood.

18
Procedures What's the difference between bypass and sleeve?

Key differences between procedures:

  • Sleeve: Removes 80% of stomach (restrictive only)
  • Bypass: Smaller stomach + rerouting (restrictive + malabsorptive)
  • Bypass: Better diabetes control
  • Sleeve: Less dietary restrictions
  • Bypass: More nutritional supplements needed

Best choice depends on your health conditions, BMI, and lifestyle preferences.

19
Procedures What's mini gastric bypass?

Mini gastric bypass (MGB) is a simplified alternative to traditional RYGB:

  • Single anastomosis (one connection point)
  • Shorter surgery time (60-75 minutes)
  • Faster recovery with similar weight loss results
  • Excellent for diabetes control

Not all patients qualify for MGB. We evaluate individual anatomy and health factors.

20
Procedures Can surgery be reversed?

Bypass procedures can be reversed but sleeve gastrectomy cannot:

  • Bypass reversal: Technically possible but rarely done
  • Reasons for reversal: Rare complications, medical reasons
  • Complications: Higher risk than original surgery
  • Alternatives: Revision often preferred over reversal
21
Procedures Robotic vs laparoscopic surgery — which is better?

Both approaches have advantages:

  • Robotic: 3D vision, wristed instruments, better ergonomics
  • Robotic: Better for complex cases and revisions
  • Laparoscopic: Proven track record, faster surgery
  • Laparoscopic: Lower cost

We offer both techniques. Robotic is 15-20% more expensive but provides precision.

22
Procedures What about intragastric balloon?

Intragastric balloon (IGB) is a non-surgical temporary option:

  • Duration: 6-12 months maximum
  • Weight loss: 10-15% of total body weight
  • Best for patients who aren't surgical candidates
  • Reversible with endoscopy removal
  • Less effective than surgery for long-term results

Often used as a bridge to surgery or for patients who prefer non-surgical options.

Procedures Can revision surgery be done?

Yes, revision surgery is possible for patients with complications or unsatisfactory results:

  • Revision rate: 5-10% of all bariatric surgeries
  • Common reasons: inadequate weight loss, complications, weight regain
  • Revision surgery is more complex than primary surgery
  • Requires specialized surgical expertise

We have extensive experience with revision surgeries and successful outcomes.

Recovery How painful is the recovery?

Pain is generally mild to moderate and well-controlled:

  • First 24-48 hours: Mild soreness (oral painkillers sufficient)
  • Week 1: Most patients off painkillers
  • Laparoscopic technique means minimal tissue trauma
  • Walking begins the same day as surgery

Patients often say the pain is much less than expected.

Lifestyle Will I need vitamins for life?

Yes, lifelong supplementation is essential:

  • Multivitamin: Daily for all patients (lifelong)
  • B12 + Iron: Especially after bypass
  • Calcium + Vitamin D: Daily, lifelong
  • Protein supplements: First 6-12 months

We provide a lifetime follow-up program with regular blood tests to monitor levels.

Procedures Can I have other surgeries at the same time?

Yes, certain procedures can be combined to reduce anesthesia exposure:

  • Gallbladder removal: Commonly done with bariatric surgery
  • Hernia repair: Can be combined safely
  • Appendectomy: Sometimes performed simultaneously

We evaluate each case individually to determine the safest approach.

Safety & Risk What if I have a hiatal hernia?

Hiatal hernias are common with obesity and we address them during bariatric surgery:

  • Small hernias: Repaired during the same procedure
  • Large hernias: May require specialized repair techniques
  • Often improves GERD symptoms significantly

We perform hiatal hernia repair routinely during sleeve gastrectomy and bypass procedures.
