Procedure Overview

What is Sleeve Gastrectomy?

Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy (LSG), also known as Vertical Sleeve Gastrectomy, is a streamlined bariatric and metabolic surgery that has revolutionized modern weight loss treatment. By reshaping the stomach without altering the intestinal pathway, it delivers rapid weight loss, metabolic comorbidity improvement, and an exceptionally straightforward recovery.

During the procedure, Dr. Atul Peters uses precision surgical staplers to remove approximately 75% to 80% of the outer stomach (along the greater curvature) over a calibrated sizing bougie. What remains is a sleek, vertical, banana-shaped tube or "sleeve" with a volume of roughly 100 to 150 ml. This creates a profound restrictive effect: because stomach capacity is reduced to a fraction of its original size, you feel completely satisfied after eating a small, healthy portion of food.

Just as importantly, Sleeve Gastrectomy works through a powerful hormonal and metabolic mechanism. The outer dome of the stomach that is surgically removed—the gastric fundus—is the body's primary production center for ghrelin, the hormone that triggers intense hunger, cravings, and appetite signaling in the brain. Once the fundus is removed, circulating ghrelin levels drop precipitously within 24 hours. Patients routinely report a freeing sense of mental relief as nagging hunger pangs and food noise subside.

Because the pyloric valve (the stomach's natural outlet muscle) and the entire small intestine remain completely intact, food is digested and absorbed along its normal physiological pathway. This makes Sleeve Gastrectomy an ideal choice for patients who want to avoid intestinal rerouting, minimize the risk of dumping syndrome or vitamin deficiencies, and enjoy a brief 1 to 2 night hospital stay at Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital, Saket.

The Ghrelin Advantage: Freedom from Food Noise

Willpower alone rarely overcomes biological hunger. Because the sleeve operation surgically removes the gastric fundus—the anatomical home of ghrelin—patients experience a physiological reset. Cravings diminish, satiety occurs rapidly after a few bites, and the relentless mental preoccupation with food is quieted.

Preserved Gastrointestinal Anatomy

Unlike gastric bypass, Sleeve Gastrectomy leaves the pyloric valve and small intestine untouched. Food mixes normally with digestive enzymes, iron, calcium, and vitamins along the duodenum and jejunum. This significantly lowers the risk of nutritional deficiencies and dumping syndrome while allowing a normal eating experience.

Robotic da Vinci Precision at Max Smart

Dr. Atul Peters frequently performs Sleeve Gastrectomy using the advanced da Vinci Robotic Surgical System. The 3D high-definition visualization and wristed robotic instruments enable meticulous staple-line reinforcement and sub-millimeter suturing accuracy, resulting in superior safety and a leak rate near zero.

When is Sleeve Preferred over Bypass?

Sleeve Gastrectomy is excellent for patients with Class II or Class III obesity who do not suffer from severe chronic acid reflux (Barrett's esophagus or severe GERD). It is also the preferred primary procedure for younger patients, those taking essential oral medications, or patients with previous extensive abdominal surgery.

The Procedure

How Sleeve Surgery Works

A precise, 40-minute minimally invasive procedure that reshapes your stomach for lasting weight control

01

Pre-Surgery Optimization & Safety Protocol

Your surgical journey begins with a comprehensive evaluation including body composition analysis, dietary counseling, blood work, cardiac and pulmonary clearances, and psychological review. Specialized blood clot prevention (DVT prophylaxis) is administered prior to OR transfer.

02

Minimal Access Port Placement

Under general anesthesia, Dr. Peters makes 3 to 4 tiny incisions (5–12 mm) across the upper abdomen. Through these ports, a high-definition 4K camera and specialized laparoscopic or robotic instruments are introduced without cutting major abdominal muscles.

03

Mobilizing the Greater Curvature

The surgeon carefully frees the outer curve of the stomach from its surrounding attachments and blood vessels, particularly along the gastric fundus where hunger-causing ghrelin hormones are concentrated.

04

Sizing Bougie & Stapling the Sleeve

A calibrated medical sizing tube (bougie) is inserted down the esophagus into the stomach. Using advanced surgical linear staplers, Dr. Peters divides and seals the stomach alongside the bougie, removing ~75–80% of the outer stomach tissue.

05

Staple Line Reinforcement & Leak Test

To ensure maximum safety and prevent bleeding or leaks, the long staple line is meticulously inspected and reinforced. An intraoperative leak test is conducted using methylene blue dye or air pressure under direct vision.

06

Sutureless Skin Closure & Rapid Recovery

The instruments are removed and the tiny skin incisions are sealed cleanly with medical skin glue—no external stitches, staples, or heavy bandages required. You awaken comfortably in recovery and are walking within hours.

Post-Surgery Diet

Recovery & Dietary Timeline

A structured 4-phase nutrition plan designed to let your new gastric sleeve heal safely while maximizing fat loss.

✓ Do's for Sleeve Patients

  • Sip liquids slowly throughout the day (aim for 2 to 2.5L daily)
  • Prioritize protein first at every meal (aim for 60 to 80g daily)
  • Chew solid foods thoroughly (20 to 30 times per bite)
  • Take daily bariatric multivitamins, B12, and calcium as prescribed
  • Stop eating immediately when you feel satisfied or full

✗ Don'ts for Sleeve Patients

  • Never drink carbonated or fizzy beverages (can stretch the sleeve)
  • Do not drink liquids with meals (wait 30 min before and after)
  • Avoid sugary juices, sodas, and high-calorie coffee drinks
  • Never use straws (introduces air into the sleeve, causing pain)
  • Avoid grazing or eating mindless snacks out of boredom

Frequently Asked

Sleeve Surgery Questions Answered

10 of the most common clinical and practical questions about Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy, answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

What is Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy (LSG) and how does it work?

Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy is a minimally invasive bariatric surgery where ~75-80% of the stomach is removed along its outer curve. This leaves a narrow, banana-shaped vertical tube or "sleeve" (approx. 100-150 ml capacity). It works through two mechanisms: significantly restricting portion sizes and dramatically reducing the production of ghrelin, the body's primary hunger hormone.

How does removing part of the stomach reduce hunger (the Ghrelin effect)?

The outer dome of the stomach, known as the gastric fundus, is the primary manufacturing site for ghrelin, the hormone responsible for signaling hunger and cravings to the brain. Because the fundus is permanently removed during sleeve gastrectomy, circulating ghrelin levels plummet within 24 hours post-op, leaving patients with drastically reduced hunger and an easier time adhering to healthy portion sizes.

Is Sleeve Gastrectomy reversible?

No. Unlike gastric bypass or banding, Sleeve Gastrectomy involves the permanent resection and removal of part of the stomach from the body. Because no intestinal rerouting occurs, however, the digestive tract maintains its natural pathway. If ever needed years later, a sleeve can be revised or converted into a Gastric Bypass or Banded Sleeve.

How does Sleeve Gastrectomy compare to Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass?

Sleeve Gastrectomy is a simpler operation with a shorter surgical time (~40 minutes) that does not divide or reroute the small intestine, meaning natural nutrient absorption is preserved and the risk of dumping syndrome is very low. Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass is preferred for patients with severe pre-existing acid reflux (GERD) or severe Type 2 Diabetes, as bypass rerouting provides slightly higher metabolic remission rates.

Can my stomach stretch back to its original size after Sleeve Gastrectomy?

While the stomach sleeve is living tissue and can soften or dilate slightly over 5 to 10 years, it will never grow back to its original pre-surgery size. To prevent clinically significant dilation and protect long-term weight loss, patients must avoid frequent grazing, drinking carbonated beverages, or regularly eating past the point of fullness. For patients seeking permanent protection against stretching, Dr. Peters also offers Banded Sleeve Gastrectomy.

How many days will I stay in the hospital after a Sleeve surgery?

Most patients stay just 1 to 2 nights at Max Smart Hospital, Saket. Because the procedure is performed laparoscopically or robotically through tiny 5-12 mm incisions closed with medical glue, post-operative discomfort is minimal, and patients are up and walking within a few hours of surgery.

Will I experience acid reflux or heartburn after Sleeve Gastrectomy?

Because the sleeve creates a higher-pressure gastric tube, some patients may experience temporary mild heartburn during the first few weeks of recovery, which is easily managed with routine antacid medications (PPIs). However, for patients who suffer from severe, chronic GERD prior to surgery, Dr. Peters typically recommends Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass instead, as bypass is an effective anti-reflux operation.

What is the diet progression after Sleeve Gastrectomy (liquids to solids)?

Your dietary recovery follows four structured phases: Weeks 1-2 consist of clear liquids and hydration; Week 3 introduces full liquids and protein shakes; Weeks 4-5 transition to soft, pureed foods; and Week 6+ marks a gradual return to a solid, nutrient-dense diet emphasizing high protein, thorough chewing, and daily multivitamin supplementation.

Can Sleeve Gastrectomy be performed robotically with the da Vinci system?

Yes. Dr. Atul Peters is a Centre of Excellence certified robotic surgeon and routinely performs da Vinci Robotic Sleeve Gastrectomy. The robotic system provides 10x 3D magnification, tremor filtration, and articulating wrists, allowing superior precision when suturing and reinforcing the long staple line.
