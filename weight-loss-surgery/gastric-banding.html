Procedure Overview

What is Gastric Banding (LAGB)?

Laparoscopic Adjustable Gastric Banding (LAGB) is a minimally invasive bariatric surgery designed for patients seeking significant weight reduction through portion control without altering their natural digestive anatomy. It is uniquely adjustable, fully reversible, and carries one of the lowest surgical risk profiles in bariatric medicine.

During the procedure, Dr. Atul Peters places a soft, biocompatible silicone collar around the uppermost portion of the stomach, just below the gastroesophageal junction. This divides the stomach into two interconnected chambers: a small upper pouch (holding approximately 30 to 50 ml of food) and the larger remaining lower stomach. The band acts as a gentle bottleneck, allowing food to pass slowly from the upper pouch into the digestive tract below.

The defining technological feature of the gastric band is its adjustability. The inner surface of the silicone collar contains an inflatable balloon. This balloon is connected by thin, sterile medical tubing to a small titanium access port placed beneath the skin and fat of the abdominal wall. During simple outpatient clinic visits, Dr. Peters can access this subcutaneous port with a fine needle to add or withdraw sterile saline solution. Adding saline tightens the band (increasing restriction), while withdrawing saline loosens it—allowing precise, tailored weight loss control that adapts to your lifestyle, pregnancy, or changing medical needs.

Because no stomach tissue is cut, stapled, or removed, and no intestinal rerouting occurs, nutritional absorption remains completely normal. With over 25+ years of surgical mastery at Max Smart Super Speciality Hospital, Saket, Dr. Peters performs primary banding, ongoing band optimization, and expert bariatric revisions—helping patients achieve safe, sustainable weight control with an outpatient or overnight hospital stay.

Complete Patient Guide

Laparoscopic Gastric Banding in Delhi: Complete Guide

For patients who desire meaningful surgical weight loss without permanent alterations to their digestive anatomy, Laparoscopic Adjustable Gastric Banding offers a flexible, reversible pathway backed by Dr. Atul Peters' comprehensive surgical care.

How the Access Port Works

The subcutaneous port is attached securely to your abdominal muscle fascia beneath the skin. During regular clinic follow-ups, sterile saline is injected into the port to gently inflate the band's inner balloon. This fine-tuning allows Dr. Peters to calibrate your rate of weight loss while preventing nausea or food blockage.

Zero Stomach Cutting & Maximum Safety

Because gastric banding does not involve surgical stapling, cutting of stomach tissue, or intestinal anastomosis, the risk of serious perioperative complications like staple-line leaks or internal bleeding is virtually zero. Your stomach remains anatomically whole and healthy.

Who Should Choose a Gastric Band?

Gastric banding is particularly favored by patients with Class I or II obesity (BMI 30–40) who want a less invasive, reversible option, women planning future pregnancies (where band restriction can be temporarily loosened), or older patients who may be higher risk for longer operations.

Expertise in Band Revisions & Conversions

While many patients enjoy long-term success with banding, some may experience band intolerance or weight regain over a decade. Dr. Peters is a recognized master in bariatric revision surgery, safely removing older bands and converting patients to a Sleeve Gastrectomy or Bypass with outstanding metabolic results.

The Procedure

How Gastric Banding Works

A step-by-step walkthrough of Dr. Atul Peters' 30-minute minimal access banding technique

01

Pre-Operative Evaluation

Comprehensive evaluation by Dr. Peters' multidisciplinary team. Nutritional counseling emphasizes the importance of thorough chewing and mindful portion sizes to prepare you for life with an adjustable band.

02

Keyhole Laparoscopic Access

Under general anesthesia, 3 to 4 tiny incisions (5–10 mm) are made across the abdomen. High-definition 4K laparoscopic cameras and precision specialized instruments are inserted with minimal tissue trauma.

03

Creating the Retrogastric Tunnel

Using atraumatic dissection, the surgeon creates a small, precise pathway (tunnel) behind the upper stomach, just below the gastroesophageal junction, ensuring no blood vessels or nerves are injured.

04

Positioning & Locking the Band

The silicone adjustable band is passed through the retrogastric tunnel and wrapped securely around the upper stomach. The band's buckle mechanism is locked into place, establishing the new 30–50 ml upper stomach pouch.

05

Gastro-Gastric Suturing (Anti-Slippage)

To permanently prevent the band from sliding or migrating over time, Dr. Peters sutures a fold of lower stomach tissue over the anterior band ring (gastro-gastric plication), anchoring it firmly in its ideal position.

06

Subcutaneous Port Placement & Closure

The tubing is connected to a titanium access port, which is sutured securely to the abdominal muscle fascia beneath the skin. Incisions are closed with cosmetic skin glue, allowing rapid discharge within 24 hours.

Post-Surgery Diet

Recovery & Dietary Rules

Because the opening created by the band is narrow, mastering thorough chewing and mindful sipping is essential for success.

✓ Do's for Gastric Band Patients

  • Chew every bite of solid food 25 to 30 times until liquid before swallowing
  • Eat small, nutrient-dense meals slowly over 20 to 30 minutes
  • Stop eating the exact moment you feel gentle fullness in your upper chest
  • Sip 2 liters of water daily between meals to stay fully hydrated
  • Attend all scheduled clinic visits for timely saline port adjustments

✗ Don'ts for Gastric Band Patients

  • Never swallow large chunks of meat, bread, or fibrous vegetables
  • Do not drink liquids while eating solid meals (wait 30 min after)
  • Avoid carbonated drinks or fizzy sodas (causes pouch pressure)
  • Avoid high-calorie liquid sweets (milkshakes, ice cream melt right through)
  • Never lie down immediately after eating a solid meal

Frequently Asked

Gastric Banding Questions Answered

10 of the most common clinical and practical questions about Laparoscopic Adjustable Gastric Banding (LAGB), answered by Dr. Atul Peters.

What is Laparoscopic Adjustable Gastric Banding (LAGB)?

Laparoscopic Adjustable Gastric Banding (LAGB) is a minimally invasive, purely restrictive bariatric procedure. A medical-grade silicone ring with an inner inflatable balloon is placed around the upper portion of the stomach. This divides the stomach into a small upper pouch and a larger lower pouch, creating a narrow passage that slows down food emptying and promotes profound, lasting satiety from very small portions.

How does the adjustable silicone band help me lose weight?

The band acts as a physical restriction. Because the upper stomach pouch can only hold about 1 to 2 ounces of food at a time, you feel completely full within just a few bites. Furthermore, the slow trickling of food through the narrow opening stimulates vagal stretch receptors, sending sustained fullness signals to the brain for hours after eating.

How are band adjustments (fills and defills) performed in the clinic?

The silicone band is connected by a thin, flexible tubing to a small access port placed securely beneath the abdominal skin. During a routine outpatient clinic visit, Dr. Peters or his clinical team accesses this port with a fine needle to add or remove sterile saline solution. Adding saline inflates the band (tightening restriction), while removing saline deflates it (loosening restriction).

Is any part of my stomach cut or removed during gastric banding?

No. Unlike Sleeve Gastrectomy or Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass, gastric banding involves zero stomach cutting, zero stapling, and zero intestinal rerouting. Your natural gastrointestinal anatomy remains 100% intact and undisturbed throughout the procedure.

What is the average weight loss with an adjustable gastric band?

Patients undergoing adjustable gastric banding typically achieve an average of 40% to 50% excess weight loss (EWL) over 18 to 24 months. While weight loss occurs more gradually than with bypass or sleeve surgery, steady compliance with diet and routine band adjustments ensures reliable long-term weight reduction.

What happens if I get pregnant or need surgery later? Can the band be loosened?

Yes! One of the greatest advantages of adjustable gastric banding is its dynamic adaptability. If a patient becomes pregnant, experiences temporary illness, or requires increased nutritional intake for any medical reason, the band can be completely deflated in the clinic in minutes without any surgical intervention.

Can an adjustable gastric band be removed or converted to a Sleeve or Bypass?

Yes. Because the stomach is not cut or altered during initial banding, the procedure is fully reversible. If a patient experiences band intolerance, slippage, or inadequate weight loss over the years, Dr. Atul Peters specializes in laparoscopically removing the band and converting the patient to a Sleeve Gastrectomy or Roux-en-Y Bypass in a single or two-stage operation.

Why is chewing solid food thoroughly so important with a gastric band?

Because the opening between the upper stomach pouch and the lower stomach is narrow (roughly the diameter of a dime when adjusted), solid food must be chewed to a smooth, liquid paste before swallowing. Chewing each bite 25 to 30 times prevents food particles from getting lodged in the band opening, which could cause discomfort or regurgitation.

How long does the banding surgery take and what is the recovery time?

Laparoscopic gastric banding is one of the quickest bariatric procedures, taking approximately 30 to 40 minutes to perform under general anesthesia. Because there are no stomach staple lines or bowel incisions, recovery is remarkably rapid. Most patients return home within 24 hours and resume normal daily activities within 3 to 5 days.

Yes. When performed for clinically indicated obesity (BMI ≥ 35, or BMI ≥ 32.5 with metabolic comorbidities like Type 2 Diabetes or sleep apnea), gastric banding is covered under IRDA-approved bariatric health insurance policies across India.
