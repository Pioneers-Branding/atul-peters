Understanding The Procedure

How Ileal Interposition Works

Laparoscopic Ileal Interposition is often referred to as **"Neuroendocrine Surgery"** because it targets the gastrointestinal hormone system rather than mechanical restriction or malabsorption. It represents the pinnacle of physiological diabetes reversal.

The L-Cell Repositioning Concept

The distal small intestine (ileum) contains the highest density of specialized endocrine cells called **L-cells**. Normally, it takes 2 to 4 hours for food to reach the ileum. In Ileal Interposition, a 100–150 cm segment of the ileum is surgically moved (interposed) into the upper small intestine just below the stomach.

Immediate Incretin Hormone Explosion

Because the L-cell rich ileum is now at the top of the digestive tract, unabsorbed food hits it within minutes of eating! This triggers an immediate, massive release of natural **GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1)** and **PYY** hormones into the bloodstream after every meal.

Internal GLP-1 Therapy Without Malabsorption

This natural hormone surge acts like a powerful internal GLP-1 medication—stimulating pancreatic beta-cells to secrete insulin, suppressing glucose-raising glucagon, and reversing cellular insulin resistance. Crucially, because no intestine is bypassed or removed, **total nutrient absorption remains 100% intact**.

Candidate Screening

Are You an Ideal Candidate for Ileal Interposition?

According to international diabetes surgery consensus (DSS-II), Ileal Interposition is the gold-standard recommendation for individuals with uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes and a **BMI of 27.5 to 35 kg/m²** (Asian criteria) who have adequate pancreatic beta-cell reserve (confirmed by C-peptide testing) and wish to achieve glycemic cure without undergoing aggressive weight loss or intestinal malabsorption.

Ask Dr. Peters
Why is Ileal Interposition called "Neuroendocrine Surgery"?

Standard bariatric surgeries work primarily through mechanical restriction (smaller stomach) or caloric malabsorption (bypassing intestine). **Ileal Interposition** is called neuroendocrine surgery because it specifically targets the gastrointestinal endocrine system. By repositioning the L-cell rich ileum to the upper digestive tract, it alters hormonal signaling (massively boosting GLP-1 and PYY) to cure diabetes without relying on forced starvation or malabsorption.

Can I undergo this surgery if my BMI is only 28 or 30 kg/m²?

Yes! This is the exact patient profile Ileal Interposition was designed for. In Asian populations, Type 2 Diabetes often develops at lower BMI thresholds (**BMI 27.5 to 35 kg/m²**). Because Ileal Interposition preserves 100% of your intestinal absorptive length and does not restrict the stomach as aggressively as traditional gastric bypass, you achieve diabetes remission without losing excessive or unhealthy amounts of weight.

Why is there zero malabsorption with Ileal Interposition?

In traditional bypass surgeries like Roux-en-Y or biliopancreatic diversion, long sections of the small intestine are bypassed and excluded from the flow of food. In Ileal Interposition, **no intestine is bypassed or removed from the food stream**—a segment of the ileum is simply moved upstream in sequence. Because all food still passes through the entire length of your small intestine, your body absorbs 100% of its proteins, vitamins, and minerals.

How soon will my blood sugar levels normalize?

The hormonal reset begins immediately when food contacts the repositioned ileum. Most patients see a dramatic drop in blood glucose within **24 to 48 hours after surgery**. Over 85% to 90% of patients are able to completely stop insulin injections and oral diabetes medications within days or weeks, maintaining normal HbA1c levels long-term.

Is Ileal Interposition covered by health insurance in India?

Yes. Under **IRDAI guidelines**, metabolic surgical procedures for Type 2 Diabetes are covered by health insurance policies in India for individuals meeting BMI and comorbidity criteria (such as BMI ≥ 32.5 kg/m² with uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes). Max Hospital offers seamless cashless processing with all major insurance networks and TPAs.

What is the recovery timeline after laparoscopic Ileal Interposition?

Performed laparoscopically or robotically through 4 to 5 small keyhole incisions, recovery is smooth and comfortable. Patients walk within hours of surgery, stay in the hospital for **2 to 3 nights**, and can return to routine non-strenuous desk work and daily activities within 7 to 10 days.
