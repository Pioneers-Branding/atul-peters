Understanding The Procedure

How Duodenojejunal Bypass Works

Laparoscopic Duodenojejunal Bypass (DJB) is a refined metabolic operation designed to target the physiological root cause of Type 2 Diabetes. Unlike traditional bypass surgeries that create a tiny stomach pouch, DJB preserves your natural stomach volume and outflow valve.

The Pylorus Advantage

In standard Roux-en-Y gastric bypass, the pyloric valve is bypassed, which can sometimes cause food to empty too rapidly into the small intestine (dumping syndrome). In DJB, the duodenum is transected just below the intact pylorus. This maintains normal, controlled gastric emptying and prevents reactive hypoglycemia and nausea.

Dual Hormonal Reset (Foregut & Hindgut)

By excluding the duodenum (foregut exclusion), the secretion of anti-incretin signals is halted. Simultaneously, bringing a loop of jejunum up to the duodenum delivers nutrients rapidly to the lower intestine (hindgut stimulation), triggering an immediate surge in natural GLP-1 and PYY hormones.

Weight-Independent Remission

Because of this profound incretin hormone reset, cellular insulin resistance is broken almost instantly. Patients experience normalized fasting blood sugar and reduced insulin requirements within 24 to 48 hours—demonstrating a direct metabolic cure independent of weight loss.

Are You an Ideal Candidate for DJB?

According to international consensus guidelines (DSS-II and IFSO/ASMBS), Duodenojejunal Bypass is highly recommended for individuals with Type 2 Diabetes and a BMI ≥ 27.5 kg/m² (Asian criteria) whose blood sugar remains inadequately controlled with medications, or who specifically desire a pylorus-preserving metabolic procedure without dumping risks.

Ask Dr. Peters
What is the exact difference between DJB and Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass?

In standard Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (RYGB), the surgeon creates a small egg-sized stomach pouch and bypasses the pyloric valve. In **Duodenojejunal Bypass (DJB)**, your natural stomach volume and pyloric valve are preserved; the intestinal bypass is performed just below the pylorus in the duodenum. This maintains normal stomach emptying while still achieving the powerful incretin hormone surge that reverses diabetes.

Why does preserving the pyloric valve prevent dumping syndrome?

Dumping syndrome occurs when food (especially carbohydrates and sugars) moves too rapidly from the stomach into the small intestine without being properly digested. Because DJB keeps your natural pyloric valve intact, your stomach continues to release food into the intestine at a normal, controlled rate, virtually eliminating dumping symptoms like sweating, nausea, dizziness, and sugar crashes.

Can I undergo DJB if my BMI is below 35 kg/m²?

Yes! According to international diabetes consensus guidelines (DSS-II and IFSO/ASMBS), metabolic surgery is recommended for Asian individuals with a **BMI ≥ 27.5 kg/m²** who have uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes despite optimal medical therapy. Because DJB does not restrict stomach volume as aggressively as other surgeries, it is exceptionally well-suited for mildly obese patients seeking glycemic cure without excessive weight loss.

How soon after DJB surgery can I stop taking insulin?

Most patients experience a dramatic drop in blood glucose levels within **24 to 48 hours after surgery**, while still in the hospital. Our endocrinology team typically reduces or completely discontinues insulin injections and oral medications before you are discharged home. Full glycemic stabilization is monitored closely over the first 3 months.

Is Duodenojejunal Bypass covered by health insurance in India?

Yes. Under **IRDAI guidelines**, metabolic and bariatric surgical procedures are covered by health insurance policies in India for individuals meeting BMI and comorbidity criteria (e.g., BMI ≥ 32.5 kg/m² with Type 2 Diabetes). Max Hospital offers cashless processing with all major insurance networks and TPAs.

What is the recovery timeline after laparoscopic DJB?

Because the procedure is performed laparoscopically or robotically through 3 to 5 tiny keyhole incisions, recovery is swift. Most patients walk within 4 hours of surgery, stay in the hospital for **1 to 2 nights**, and can return to routine non-strenuous desk work and daily activities within 5 to 7 days.
